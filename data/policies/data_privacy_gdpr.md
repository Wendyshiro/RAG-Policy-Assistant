# Data Privacy and GDPR Compliance Policy

**Document ID:** POL-010  
**Last Updated:** January 2024  
**Version:** 1.4

## 1. Purpose and Scope

This Data Privacy and GDPR Compliance Policy outlines the company's commitment to protecting personal data and complying with data protection regulations, including the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), and other applicable laws.

## 2. Definitions

### 2.1 Personal Data
Any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person, including:
- Names and contact information
- Identification numbers (SSN, passport, etc.)
- Financial information
- Health and medical information
- Educational records
- Biometric data
- Online identifiers (IP addresses, cookies)
- Location data
- Racial or ethnic origin
- Political opinions
- Religious or philosophical beliefs
- Trade union membership
- Genetic data
- Criminal records

### 2.2 Special Categories of Data
More sensitive personal data requiring additional protection:
- Health data
- Genetic data
- Biometric data
- Racial or ethnic origin
- Political opinions
- Religious beliefs
- Trade union membership
- Criminal records
- Sex life or sexual orientation

### 2.3 Data Processing
Any operation on personal data, including:
- Collection and recording
- Organization and storage
- Adaptation and alteration
- Retrieval and access
- Use and disclosure
- Erasure and destruction

## 3. Legal Basis for Data Processing

### 3.1 Consent
- Employees provide explicit consent for data processing
- Consent must be freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous
- Consent forms provided and documented
- Withdrawal of consent requires same procedures

### 3.2 Contract
- Data processing necessary for employment contract
- Processing limited to purposes required for contract
- Examples: Payroll, benefits, employment administration

### 3.3 Legal Obligation
- Processing required by law or regulation
- Tax and employment law compliance
- Court orders or legal proceedings

### 3.4 Vital Interests
- Processing necessary to protect vital interests of individual
- Health and safety situations
- Emergency situations

### 3.5 Public Task
- Processing necessary for public function or authority
- Government agencies or public services

### 3.6 Legitimate Interests
- Processing necessary for company's legitimate interests
- Balanced against individual's rights and interests
- Examples: Security, fraud prevention, customer service

## 4. Data Collection and Storage

### 4.1 Data Collection Principles
- Collect only data necessary for identified purpose
- Collect from data subject when possible
- Inform individuals of data collection at time of collection
- Provide Privacy Notice
- Limit use to stated purpose
- Collect minimal amount necessary

### 4.2 Types of Data Collected
**Employee Data:**
- Name, address, phone, email
- Date of birth
- Social security number
- Bank account information
- Tax information (W-4, I-9)
- Health insurance information
- Emergency contacts
- Performance data

**Customer Data:**
- Name, address, contact information
- Purchase history
- Payment information
- Preferences and feedback
- Communication records

**Vendor Data:**
- Company information
- Contact persons
- Financial information
- Performance metrics

### 4.3 Data Storage
- Personal data stored securely
- Access limited to authorized employees
- Encryption at rest and in transit
- Secure servers and backup systems
- Regular security testing
- Off-site backup for disaster recovery

### 4.4 Data Retention
- Retain data only as long as necessary
- Retention schedule:
  - Employee data: 7 years post-termination (for legal/tax)
  - Customer data: Duration of relationship + 3 years (legal hold)
  - Vendor data: Duration of relationship + 3 years
- Secure deletion when retention period ends
- Legal holds override normal retention

## 5. Individual Rights

### 5.1 Right of Access
- Individuals may request copy of their personal data
- Request process:
  - Submit written request to HR
  - Identity verification required
  - Response within 30 days
  - Data provided in common electronic format
- Cost: Free (unless request manifestly unfounded or excessive)

### 5.2 Right to Rectification
- Individuals may correct inaccurate data
- Request to HR or manager
- Correction made without undue delay
- Related parties notified if appropriate

### 5.3 Right to Erasure ("Right to be Forgotten")
- Under certain conditions, individuals may request deletion
- Company may deny if:
  - Data still necessary for original purpose
  - Legal obligation to retain
  - Public interest requires retention
  - Vital interests at stake
  - Need to establish/defend legal claims
- If approved: Data deleted securely
- Notification sent to data subject and recipients

### 5.4 Right to Restrict Processing
- Individuals may request limitation of processing
- Applies when:
  - Accuracy is disputed
  - Processing is unlawful
  - Data is no longer needed but required for legal claim
  - Individual objects to processing
- Restricted data stored separately
- Not deleted, but processing limited

### 5.5 Right to Data Portability
- Individuals may request data in machine-readable format
- Applies to data provided by individual or generated from individual's activity
- Common formats: CSV, JSON, XML
- Data provided within 30 days
- May request direct transfer to other provider

### 5.6 Right to Object
- Individuals may object to processing
- Applies to processing for:
  - Direct marketing
  - Legitimate interests
  - Statistical purposes
- Company must stop processing unless:
  - Legal obligation requires continued processing
  - Data necessary for legal claims

### 5.7 Right to Human Review of Automated Decisions
- Individuals may request human review of automated decisions
- Applies to decisions with legal or significant effects
- Examples: Automated hiring, performance evaluation, credit decisions
- Right to explanation of decision logic
- Right to challenge automated decision

### 6.1 Submitting a Request
Data subjects may submit requests to:

- **In-person:** HR Office, [Location]

Request should include:

- Specific data or information requested

### 6.2 Processing Requests
- HR acknowledges request within 24 hours
- Verifies identity of requester
- Locates relevant data
- Prepares response
- Responds within 30 days

### 6.3 Request Responses
- Data provided in requested format
- Explanation of decisions
- Information about appeal process
- Timeframe for implementation

### 6.4 Denial of Requests
- If request cannot be fulfilled, provide written explanation
- Explain legal grounds for denial
- Provide information about appeal process
- Appeal deadline: 30 days from denial

## 7. Data Breaches and Incident Response

### 7.1 Definition of Data Breach
Unauthorized access, disclosure, or destruction of personal data, including:
- Hacking and cyber attacks
- Loss or theft of devices
- Unauthorized employee access
- Accidental disclosure
- Ransomware or malware

### 7.2 Breach Notification
If breach occurs:
1. **Detection**: Breach identified immediately
2. **Assessment**: Severity and scope determined (within 72 hours)
3. **Notification to Authorities**: If required by law, authorities notified within 72 hours
4. **Notification to Individuals**: 
   - Notification within 30 days if high risk
   - Include: Nature of breach, data affected, likely consequences, mitigating measures, contact info
5. **Documentation**: All actions documented

### 7.3 Breach Response Procedures
1. Isolate affected systems
2. Stop ongoing breach
3. Assess scope and impact
4. Notify Privacy Officer and Legal
5. Notify law enforcement if required
6. Preserve evidence
7. Determine notification requirements
8. Implement remediation measures
9. Conduct post-incident review

### 7.4 Breach Communication
- Individuals notified in plain language
- Notification by email, letter, or phone
- Information about data affected
- Likely consequences
- Steps taken to mitigate harm
- Resources available to individuals
- Contact for more information

## 8. Third-Party Data Processors

### 8.1 Data Processing Agreements
All third parties processing personal data must:
- Execute Data Processing Agreement (DPA)
- Agree to process data only per company instructions
- Maintain confidentiality and security
- Assist with data subject requests
- Report breaches immediately
- Delete or return data upon termination

### 8.2 Vendor Assessment
Before engaging vendors:
- Assess security and privacy practices
- Review certifications (ISO 27001, SOC 2, etc.)
- Evaluate data protection measures
- Determine sub-processor arrangements
- Review contract terms
- Document assessment

### 8.3 Ongoing Vendor Management
- Annual re-assessment of vendors
- Regular security audits
- Monitoring for breaches
- Compliance with DPA terms
- Update agreements as needed

## 9. International Data Transfers

### 9.1 Transfers Outside EU/EEA
- GDPR restricts transfers outside EU/EEA
- Transfers require "adequacy decision" or appropriate safeguards
- Safeguards include:
  - Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)
  - Binding Corporate Rules (BCRs)
  - Derogations (limited circumstances)

### 9.2 Adequacy Decisions
- EU approved list of adequate countries/regions
- Transfers to adequate jurisdictions allowed
- Currently adequate: Canada, Japan, New Zealand, etc.
- US transfers: Under Schrems II and Data Privacy Framework

### 9.3 Standard Contractual Clauses
- Contracts with third parties include SCCs
- Contracts impose data protection obligations
- Same level of protection as GDPR
- Required for transfers lacking adequacy decision

## 10. Privacy Impact Assessments

### 10.1 When Required
Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) required for:
- New data processing activities
- Changes to existing processing
- New technologies (AI, biometrics, etc.)
- Use of special categories of data
- Large-scale processing
- Processing of children's data

### 10.2 DPIA Process
1. Document processing activity
2. Assess necessity and proportionality
3. Identify risks to individuals
4. Evaluate technical and organizational measures
5. Assess compliance with GDPR
6. Consult with supervisory authority if high risk
7. Document findings and mitigations
