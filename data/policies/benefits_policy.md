# Benefits Policy

**Document ID:** POL-006  
**Last Updated:** January 2024  
**Version:** 1.3

## 1. Overview

This Benefits Policy outlines the comprehensive benefits package offered to eligible employees. Our company is committed to providing competitive benefits that support employee health, wellness, and financial security.

## 2. Eligibility

### 2.1 Full-Time Employees
- Eligible for all benefits outlined in this policy
- Benefits begin on the first day of the month following 30 days of employment
- Benefits continue through last day of employment

### 2.2 Part-Time Employees
- Eligible if working 30+ hours per week
- Receive prorated benefits based on hours worked
- Same eligibility waiting period as full-time employees

### 2.3 Ineligible Employees
- Temporary employees
- Contract/Contingent workers
- Interns and apprentices

## 3. Health Insurance

### 3.1 Medical Insurance
The company offers multiple medical insurance plans through [Insurance Provider]:
- Employee contribution: 15% of premium
- Company contribution: 85% of premium
- Plans include preferred provider networks (PPO)
- Coverage includes preventive care at no cost

### 3.2 Plan Options
- **Bronze Plan**: Lower premium, higher deductible ($2,500)
- **Silver Plan**: Mid-tier premium and deductible ($1,500) - Most popular
- **Gold Plan**: Higher premium, lower deductible ($750)
- **Platinum Plan**: Highest premium, lowest deductible ($350)

### 3.3 Deductibles and Cost-Sharing
- In-network visits: 20% coinsurance after deductible
- Out-of-network visits: 40% coinsurance after deductible
- Out-of-pocket maximum: $7,500 individual / $15,000 family
- Preventive care: 100% covered (no deductible)

### 3.4 Prescription Drug Coverage
- Generic medications: $10 copay
- Brand medications (preferred): $35 copay
- Brand medications (non-preferred): $60 copay
- Mail-order pharmacy available for 90-day supplies

## 4. Dental Insurance

### 4.1 Coverage Details
- Dental insurance provided through [Insurance Provider]
- Employee contribution: 30% of premium
- Company contribution: 70% of premium

### 4.2 Coverage Limits
- Preventive care (cleanings, exams, X-rays): 100% covered
- Basic care (fillings, extractions): 80% covered
- Major care (crowns, bridges, root canals): 50% covered
- Orthodontia: 50% covered, lifetime maximum $2,000
- Annual maximum benefit: $1,500 per person

## 5. Vision Insurance

### 5.1 Coverage Details
- Vision insurance provided through [Insurance Provider]
- Employee contribution: 25% of premium
- Company contribution: 75% of premium

### 5.2 Coverage Limits
- Eye exams: Covered in full once per year
- Frames: $150 allowance every 2 years
- Lenses: Covered in full (plastic or glass)
- Contact lenses: $150 allowance in place of frames

### 5.3 Covered Providers
- Extensive network of optometrists and ophthalmologists
- Coverage for online vision retailers (Warby Parker, Zenni, etc.)

## 6. Mental Health and Wellness

### 6.1 Mental Health Coverage
- Mental health services covered at same rate as medical
- Therapy sessions: 20% coinsurance after deductible
- Psychiatry: Covered with prior authorization
- No separate deductible for mental health

### 6.2 Employee Assistance Program (EAP)
- Free, confidential counseling service
- 5 free sessions per year per employee
- Available 24/7 via phone and online
- Covers personal, family, and work-related issues
- Phone: 1-800-XXX-XXXX

### 6.3 Wellness Programs
- Annual health screenings (free)
- Subsidized gym memberships (up to $30/month)
- Meditation and yoga classes (free on-campus)
- Health challenges and incentives ($500/year wellness credit)

## 7. Life Insurance

### 7.1 Basic Life Insurance
- Company-paid: 2x annual salary
- Automatic coverage at no cost to employee
- Coverage begins on first day of employment
- Included in benefits package

### 7.2 Supplemental Life Insurance
- Employee may purchase additional coverage
- Available in increments: $50,000, $100,000, $200,000
- Rates depend on age and health
- Guaranteed issue up to $200,000

### 7.3 Accidental Death and Dismemberment (AD&D)
- Included with basic life insurance
- Covers accidents resulting in death or disability

## 8. Disability Insurance

### 8.1 Short-Term Disability (STD)
- Coverage: 66.67% of salary
- Waiting period: 7 days (paid by company)
- Duration: Up to 12 weeks (26 weeks for maternity/paternity)
- Company-paid benefit

### 8.2 Long-Term Disability (LTD)
- Coverage: 66.67% of salary (capped at $10,000/month)
- Waiting period: 90 days
- Duration: Until age 65 or recovery
- Company-paid benefit

### 8.3 Filing a Claim
- Contact HR for claim forms
- Must have physician documentation
- Claims reviewed within 10 business days
- Appeal process available if claim denied

## 9. 401(k) Retirement Plan

### 9.1 Plan Overview
- Company-sponsored 401(k) plan
- Administered by [Plan Administrator]
- Employees may contribute up to IRS limit ($23,000 for 2024)

### 9.2 Company Match
- Company matches 100% of contributions up to 3% of salary
- Additional matching 50% on next 2% of contributions
- Maximum company match: 4% of salary
- Match vests immediately (100% vested upon contribution)

### 9.3 Vesting Schedule
- Employee contributions: Immediately vested
- Company contributions: 
  - Year 1: 25% vested
  - Year 2: 50% vested
  - Year 3: 75% vested
  - Year 4+: 100% vested

### 9.4 Investment Options
- Self-directed brokerage account available
- Target-date funds (age-based)
- Index funds (S&P 500, International, Bonds)
- Money market funds
- Employer stock option

### 9.5 Enrollment and Management
- Enroll online through [Plan Portal]
- Quarterly performance reviews
- Annual rebalancing recommended
- Financial planning resources available

## 10. Health Savings Account (HSA)

### 10.1 HSA Eligibility
- Available to employees enrolled in high-deductible health plans (HDHP)
- Bronze and Silver plans eligible

### 10.2 Contributions
- Employee contributions: Tax-deductible
- Company contribution: $500 individual / $1,000 family
- Contributions cap: $4,150 individual / $8,300 family (2024)

### 10.3 Usage
- Use for qualified medical expenses
- Unused funds roll over year-to-year (not "use-it-or-lose-it")
- After age 65, withdraw for any purpose (with taxes on non-medical)

## 11. Flexible Spending Account (FSA)

### 11.1 FSA Overview
- Pre-tax account for healthcare and dependent care expenses
- Reduces taxable income
- Annual election required during open enrollment

### 11.2 Healthcare FSA
- Annual contribution limit: $3,300 (2024)
- Use for copays, deductibles, and eligible medical expenses
- Use-it-or-lose-it: Unused funds forfeited at year-end
- $640 carryover allowed (grace period option)

### 11.3 Dependent Care FSA
- For childcare and adult dependent care
- Annual limit: $5,000 per family
- Use for preschool, after-school, summer camps, adult day care
- Reduces childcare costs by approximately 25-30%

## 12. Commuter Benefits

- Parking: Up to $315/month (2024 limit)
- Vanpool: Up to $315/month (2024 limit)

### 12.2 Flexible Commute Options

## 13. Paid Family Leave

### 13.1 Maternity Leave
- Eligible pregnant employees: 16 weeks paid leave
- Additional 4 weeks unpaid leave available
- Job protection guaranteed
- Health insurance continues during leave

### 13.2 Paternity Leave
- Eligible partners/spouses: 8 weeks paid leave
- Additional 4 weeks unpaid leave available
- Applies to biological and adoptive parents
- Non-discriminatory policy

### 13.3 Adoption Leave
- 8 weeks paid leave for adopting parents
- Applicable to domestic and international adoption
- Eligibility: Must have worked 12 months; employed at time of placement

### 13.4 Parental Leave Process
1. Notify HR at least 30 days before expected date
2. Complete FMLA paperwork if applicable
3. Health insurance continues under existing terms
4. Return-to-work meeting with HR upon return

## 17. Open Enrollment and Changes

### 17.1 Open Enrollment Period
- Occurs annually in October/November
- New employees enroll within 30 days of hire
- Changes take effect: January 1 (annual) or within 30 days (new hire)
- Benefits portal: [Portal URL]

### 17.2 Qualifying Life Events
Employees may change benefits outside open enrollment if:
- Birth or adoption of child
- Marriage or domestic partnership
- Divorce or dissolution
- Death of spouse or dependent
- Loss of coverage (COBRA ends)
- Change in employment status
- Significant change in insurance costs/coverage

Must notify HR within 30 days of life event.

### 17.3 COBRA Continuation Coverage
- Employees can continue coverage after termination
- Duration: Up to 18 months
- Cost: 100% of premium + 2% admin fee
- Notification provided at termination

## 18. Benefit Continuation and Termination

### 18.1 Benefits at Termination
- Health insurance: Ends last day of employment
- 401(k): Vested balance remains in account
- Life insurance: Portability option available
- Accrued PTO: Paid out in final paycheck

### 18.2 COBRA Rights
- Employees entitled to continue health insurance
- Notice provided at time of termination
- 60 days to elect COBRA coverage
- Retroactive coverage available
