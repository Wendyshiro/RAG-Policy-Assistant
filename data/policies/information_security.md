# Information Security Policy

**Document ID:** POL-005  
**Last Updated:** January 2024  
**Version:** 2.3

## 1. Overview and Purpose

This Information Security Policy establishes minimum standards and procedures for protecting company and customer information assets. All employees, contractors, and third parties with access to company systems are responsible for maintaining the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of company information.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:
- All employees (full-time, part-time, temporary)
- Contractors and consultants
- Vendors and third-party service providers
- All information and systems owned or operated by the company
- All devices and networks used for company business

## 3. Information Classification

### 3.1 Confidential Information
Confidential information includes data that, if disclosed, could significantly harm the company or its stakeholders:
- Customer and employee personal information
- Financial records and reports
- Business strategies and plans
- Proprietary research and development
- Source code and technical documentation
- Passwords and authentication credentials
- Security vulnerabilities and test results

### 3.2 Internal Use Only
Information that can be shared internally but should not be disclosed externally:
- Internal policies and procedures
- Organizational structure and roles
- Non-public financial metrics
- Internal project details and timelines
- Draft documents and proposals

### 3.3 Public Information
Information that has been approved for public release:
- Marketing materials and press releases
- Published research and case studies
- Product documentation
- Public website content

## 4. Access Control and Authentication

### 4.1 User Access
- Access to systems and data should be based on job role and "need to know"
- Access should be requested through the IT department
- Managers must approve all access requests
- Access should be reviewed and renewed annually
- Unused accounts should be disabled promptly

### 4.2 Passwords and Authentication
All users must:
- Use strong passwords (minimum 12 characters, mixing uppercase, lowercase, numbers, special characters)
- Never share passwords with colleagues
- Change passwords every 90 days
- Never write down or store passwords in unsecured locations
- Immediately report suspected password compromises

### 4.3 Multi-Factor Authentication
Multi-factor authentication (MFA) is required for:
- Email and messaging systems
- VPN and remote access
- Cloud applications (Google Workspace, Microsoft 365, etc.)
- Any system with sensitive data access
- Administrative and privileged accounts

### 4.4 Remote Access
Employees accessing company systems remotely must:
- Use the company VPN
- Use approved devices only
- Maintain updated antivirus and security software
- Use secure, password-protected WiFi
- Lock devices when unattended
- Never leave authenticated sessions open

## 5. Data Protection and Privacy

### 5.1 Personal Data
All personal data (employee, customer, or third-party) must be:
- Collected only for legitimate business purposes
- Used only as disclosed to the individual
- Protected with appropriate security measures
- Retained only as long as necessary
- Deleted or anonymized when no longer needed

### 5.2 Customer Data
Customer data is confidential and must be:
- Protected with strong encryption
- Accessed only by authorized employees
- Monitored for unauthorized access
- Reported if breached
- Deleted per customer request

### 5.3 Payment Card Data
If processing credit cards, the company must comply with:
- Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard (PCI DSS)
- Encryption requirements
- Regular security assessments
- Vendor management protocols

### 5.4 Health Information
Health-related information (if any is collected) must comply with:
- HIPAA regulations (if applicable)
- State privacy laws
- Strong encryption and access controls
- Limited access only to authorized personnel

## 6. Device and Network Security

### 6.1 Laptops and Computers
All company-provided devices must have:
- Operating system with latest security patches
- Antivirus and anti-malware software
- Host firewall enabled
- Full disk encryption
- Automatic lock (5-minute timeout)
- Password protection at startup

### 6.2 Mobile Devices
Company-issued mobile devices must:
- Have device management software installed
- Have a strong PIN or biometric lock
- Have encryption enabled
- Have remote wipe capability configured
- Have approved apps only
- Not be jailbroken or rooted

### 6.3 Personal Devices
Personal devices used for work must:
- Have security software installed
- Be encrypted
- Have strong authentication
- Be subject to IT management policies
- Not be shared with family members

### 6.4 Wireless Networks
Employees must:
- Use only secure, password-protected WiFi
- Avoid public WiFi for sensitive work
- Use VPN when on public networks
- Not share WiFi passwords
- Disable WiFi when not in use

## 7. Incident Response and Reporting

### 7.1 Security Incidents
A security incident is any unauthorized access, use, or disclosure of company information. This includes:
- Unauthorized access to systems or data
- Loss or theft of devices or documents
- Discovery of malware or viruses
- Suspicious emails or phishing attempts
- Data breaches or leaks
- System outages or attacks

### 7.2 Reporting Procedures
Employees who discover a security incident must:
1. Stop any ongoing compromise immediately
2. Notify the IT Security team immediately: security@company.com or (555) 123-5678
3. Do not attempt to investigate or remediate without guidance
4. Preserve evidence (screenshots, logs, etc.)
5. Maintain confidentiality about the incident

### 7.3 Incident Investigation
The IT Security team will:
- Assess the scope and severity of the incident
- Investigate the cause and impact
- Notify affected individuals if required by law
- Implement remediation measures
- Report findings to management and legal as appropriate

### 7.4 Breach Notification
If a data breach occurs:
- Affected individuals will be notified if required by law
- Notification will include what data was compromised and recommended actions
- The company will cooperate with law enforcement if needed
- Public disclosure may be required depending on jurisdiction and data type

## 8. Email and Communication Security

### 8.1 Email Usage
- Email is a business communication tool and is not private
- All email is subject to monitoring and review by the company
- Confidential information should not be sent via unencrypted email
- Use secure email protocols for sensitive data
- Do not open attachments from unknown senders
- Report phishing attempts immediately

### 8.2 Phishing and Social Engineering
Employees should be alert to:
- Emails requesting passwords or sensitive information
- Urgent requests from unknown senders
- Too-good-to-be-true offers
- Requests that seem unusual or break normal procedures
- Links or attachments from unexpected sources

Suspected phishing should be reported to IT immediately.

### 8.3 Email Retention
- Company email is retained according to document retention policies
- Employees should not delete emails to avoid legal holds
- Personal use of email should be minimal

## 9. Cloud and Software as a Service (SaaS)

### 9.1 Approved Cloud Services
Only company-approved cloud services should be used:
- Google Workspace (Gmail, Drive, Docs, etc.)
- Microsoft 365 (Outlook, Teams, OneDrive, etc.)
- Approved project management and collaboration tools
- Approved file sharing services

### 9.2 Unapproved Services
Employees must not use:
- Personal cloud storage for work files (Dropbox, personal Google Drive, iCloud, etc.)
- Unapproved file sharing services
- Unauthorized communication tools
- Personal email for work purposes

### 9.3 SaaS Security Requirements
All cloud services used must have:
- Encryption in transit and at rest
- Strong authentication and access controls
- Regular security updates
- Compliance with relevant regulations
- Data retention and deletion policies

## 10. Third-Party and Vendor Management

### 10.1 Vendor Assessment
Before engaging vendors or third parties with access to company data:
- Assess their security practices
- Review their privacy policies
- Require security certifications (SOC 2, ISO 27001, etc.)
- Conduct security questionnaires
- Verify insurance coverage

### 10.2 Vendor Agreements
Contracts with vendors accessing company data must include:
- Data protection and confidentiality requirements
- Security standards and certifications
- Incident notification requirements
- Audit and monitoring rights
- Data return or destruction requirements
- Liability and indemnification clauses

### 10.3 Vendor Monitoring
Vendors must be:
- Monitored for continued compliance
- Re-assessed periodically
- Required to report security incidents
- Subject to audits as deemed necessary

## 11. Acceptable Use Policy

### 11.1 General Acceptable Use
Company systems and resources should be used:
- Primarily for business purposes
- In compliance with all policies
- Legally and ethically
- Consistent with company values

### 11.2 Unacceptable Use
Prohibited uses of company systems include:
- Accessing, downloading, or distributing illegal content
- Harassing or threatening others
- Violating intellectual property rights
- Spreading malware or viruses
- Unauthorized system access or modification
- Excessive personal use
- Violating others' privacy

### 11.3 Monitoring and Enforcement
The company may monitor:
- Network traffic and bandwidth usage
- Email content and attachments
- Website visits and downloads
- File access and transfers
- Application usage and logins

Violations may result in disciplinary action up to termination.

## 12. Encryption and Cryptography

### 12.1 Data Encryption
Sensitive data must be encrypted:
- In transit (HTTPS, TLS, VPN)
- At rest (full disk encryption, database encryption)
- When transmitted over email or file sharing services
- When stored on portable devices

### 12.2 Encryption Keys
Encryption keys must be:
- Securely generated and stored
- Protected from unauthorized access
- Regularly rotated
- Properly disposed of when no longer needed

## 13. Backup and Disaster Recovery

### 13.1 Data Backup
Critical data is backed up:
- Daily or more frequently as needed
- To multiple locations
- With encryption
- With regular testing and verification

### 13.2 Recovery Procedures
In case of data loss or system failure:
- IT will restore data from backups
- Recovery time objective (RTO) is 24 hours for critical systems
- Recovery point objective (RPO) is daily
- Employees should report data loss immediately

## 14. Software and Patch Management

### 14.1 Approved Software
Employees should use only:
- Company-provided software
- Company-approved commercial software
- Open source software approved by IT
- Never install unauthorized software

### 14.2 Updates and Patches
All systems must:
- Install security updates promptly
- Apply operating system patches within 30 days
- Keep antivirus definitions current
- Not disable security updates

## 15. Training and Awareness

### 15.1 Security Training
All employees must complete:
- Annual security awareness training
- Role-specific security training
- Incident response procedures
- Password management training

### 15.2 Compliance
Employees are responsible for:
- Understanding security policies
- Attending required training
- Keeping knowledge current
- Reporting policy violations
- Asking questions about unclear policies

## 17. Policy Violations

### 17.1 Consequences
Violations of this Information Security Policy may result in:
- Verbal or written warning
- Suspension of system access
- Disciplinary action
- Termination of employment
- Civil or criminal prosecution (for serious violations)

### 17.2 Investigation
Security violations will be:
- Investigated thoroughly
- Documented completely
- Reported to appropriate parties
- Remediated to prevent recurrence

- Annually at minimum
- When significant security events occur
- When new threats emerge
- When new technologies are adopted
- When legal or regulatory changes occur
