# Attendance and Punctuality Policy

**Document ID:** POL-009  
**Last Updated:** January 2024  
**Version:** 1.5

## 1. Purpose

This Attendance and Punctuality Policy establishes expectations for employee attendance and timeliness. Regular attendance and punctuality are essential for maintaining productivity, team morale, and business continuity.

## 2. Attendance Expectations

### 2.1 General Requirements
- Full-time employees are expected to work their full scheduled hours
- Standard work schedule: 40 hours per week (8 hours per day, 5 days per week)
- Actual schedule may vary by department or role
- Managers communicate specific work schedules to employees

### 2.2 Schedule Variations
- Some roles may have flexible schedules (by manager approval)
- Some roles may have compressed schedules (4 ten-hour days)
- Some roles may have staggered shifts
- All variations must be approved in writing by management

### 2.3 Notification of Changes
- If work schedule will change, notify manager as soon as possible
- Changes require management approval
- Changes documented in writing
- Implementation date specified

## 3. Tardiness and Attendance Tracking

### 3.1 Punctuality Expectations
- Employees should arrive on time for their scheduled shift
- "On time" means ready to work at the start of shift
- Arriving 1-5 minutes early is encouraged
- Chronic tardiness will result in disciplinary action

### 3.2 Tracking Attendance
- Attendance is tracked using:
  - Time clock or timekeeping system
  - Manager sign-in sheets
  - Email or Slack status
  - Badge access records
- Attendance records maintained for 7+ years

### 3.3 What Counts as "Present"
- Time physically present at work location
- Authorized remote work time
- Approved off-site business activities
- Approved paid time off
- Company-approved absences

### 3.4 What Counts as "Absent"
- Unscheduled absence from work
- Failure to notify manager
- Tardiness exceeding 15 minutes
- Early departure without approval
- Unauthorized absence during shift

## 4. Absences and Excused Time Off

### 4.1 Approved Absences
Excused absences include:
- Scheduled PTO and vacation
- Sick leave
- Family medical leave (FMLA)
- Bereavement leave
- Jury duty
- Military service
- Approved personal leave
- Company-approved absences

### 4.2 Unexcused Absences
Unexcused absences include:
- No-call no-show (no notification to manager)
- Calling in with insufficient notice
- Absence without approval
- Leaving early without permission
- Repeated tardiness patterns

### 4.3 Absence Notification Procedures
If unable to work the scheduled shift:
1. Notify manager as soon as possible
2. Ideally before the start of shift
3. Provide reason for absence
4. Specify expected return date
5. Follow up with written notification if requested

**Call-in procedure:**
- Call directly: [Manager Phone]
- Email: [Manager Email]
- Absence Hotline: [Company Hotline]
- Minimum notice: Before start of shift

## 5. Disciplinary Action for Attendance Issues

### 5.1 Progressive Discipline
Attendance problems typically result in:
- **1st incident**: Verbal counseling and reminder of policy
- **2nd incident**: Written warning (within 12 months)
- **3rd incident**: 1-day suspension (within 12 months)
- **4th incident**: 3-day suspension or termination (within 12 months)

### 5.2 No-Call No-Show
- Unexcused absence without notification
- Serious violation of policy
- May result in immediate suspension or termination
- Typically considered grounds for dismissal after 1-2 instances

### 5.3 Chronic Absenteeism
- Pattern of repeated absences (5+ within 12 months)
- May result in disciplinary action independent of formal structure
- Conversation with manager required
- Performance improvement plan (PIP) may be implemented
- Termination possible for persistent pattern

### 5.4 Excessive Tardiness
- Being late on a recurring basis (3+ times within 30 days)
- Discussion with manager required
- Written warning if pattern continues
- Further discipline if behavior doesn't improve

## 6. Extended Absences

### 6.1 Planned Extended Absence
For absences exceeding 5 consecutive days:
- Request approved time off (vacation, PTO, leave)
- Medical leave: Requires medical certification
- Personal leave: Must be approved by HR and manager
- Job protection: Depends on type of leave and applicable law

### 6.2 Unplanned Extended Absence
If unable to work for extended period:
- Contact HR immediately
- Provide medical or other documentation
- Discuss options:
  - Short-term disability
  - Unpaid leave of absence
  - FMLA (if eligible)
  - Modified duty or part-time schedule

## 7. Reporting Requirements and Documentation

### 7.1 Documentation
- Each absence documented in personnel file
- Information includes: Date, reason, approved or unapproved
- Employee may request to review absence record

### 7.2 Verification of Absences
- For absences exceeding 3 consecutive days: Doctor's note required
- For suspected abuse: Medical certification may be required
- For FMLA-eligible leave: Department of Labor form completed
- Verification provided to HR, kept confidential

### 7.3 Certification and Recertification
- Initial medical certification covers one absence
- Employer may require recertification for ongoing absences
- Frequency: Once per 30 days for continuous absences
- Cost: Paid by employer

## 8. Flexibility and Accommodations

### 8.1 Flexible Scheduling
- Employees may request flexible schedules
- Requires manager and HR approval
- Must not interfere with business operations
- Subject to change if operational needs change

### 8.2 Medical Accommodations
- Employees with medical conditions may request accommodations
- Accommodation request process:
  - Provide medical documentation
  - Meet with HR
  - Discuss possible accommodations
  - Document approved accommodation
- Reasonable accommodations required under ADA

### 8.3 Family and Dependent Care
- FMLA provides 12 weeks unpaid leave for:
  - Birth and bonding of child
  - Care of spouse, child, or parent with serious health condition
  - Military caregiver leave
- Additional leave may be available under state law

## 9. Tardiness and Early Departure

### 9.1 Late Arrival Procedures
- If running late, notify manager immediately
- Explain reason and expected arrival time
- Discuss whether remote work is possible
- Time clock records arrival time
- Repeated tardiness subject to discipline

### 9.2 Early Departure
- Employee must request permission from manager
- Employer approval required to leave early
- Unapproved early departure counts as unauthorized absence
- Repeated early departures subject to discipline

### 9.3 Break and Lunch Periods
- Scheduled breaks: Typically 15 minutes (morning and afternoon)
- Lunch period: 30-60 minutes (unpaid)
- Breaks must stay within scheduled time
- Breaks documented in timekeeping system

## 10. Weather and Emergency Closures

### 10.1 Emergency Closures
In case of:
- Severe weather
- Natural disaster
- Utility failure
- Security threat

Company may:
- Close facility for the day
- Require remote work only
- Delay opening
- Close early

### 10.2 Notification of Closure
- Notification sent via email, phone, and website
- Employees checked for status on company hotline
- Local news stations (for major closures)
- Social media update
- Typically announced by 6:00 AM

### 10.3 Pay During Closure
- Full day closure: Employees paid for the day (no PTO used)
- Half day closure: Employees paid for time worked + partial day off
- Remote work available: Must work scheduled hours remotely
- Non-exempt employees: Paid for time off
- Exempt employees: Paid full salary regardless

## 11. Part-Time and Flexible Employees

### 11.1 Part-Time Attendance
- Part-time employees held to same attendance standards
- Scheduled hours: As agreed in employment contract
- Notification required if unable to work
- Same disciplinary procedures apply

### 11.2 Seasonal or Temporary Employees
- Expected to work all scheduled hours
- Early termination possible for excessive absences
- Similar attendance standards as permanent employees
- Attendance impacts renewal of contract

## 12. Remote Work and Attendance

### 12.1 Remote Work Attendance
- Remote employees must be "present" during work hours
- Available on Slack, email, and video
- Online status should reflect actual availability
- Participation in virtual meetings expected

### 12.2 No-Show for Virtual Meetings
- Missing virtual meetings without notification: Absence
- Calendar shows required participation
- Forewarning before meeting appreciated
- Pattern of absences subject to discipline

## 13. Third-Party Verification

### 13.1 Independent Medical Exam
- For pattern of suspicious absences, IME may be required
- IME paid for by employer
- Employee selects physician from approved list
- Results kept confidential
- Used to verify legitimacy of medical claims

### 13.2 Surveillance
- Company may not use surveillance to monitor individuals
- Time tracking and timekeeping systems permitted
- Badge access data permitted
- Electronic monitoring subject to state law
- Privacy expected in personal areas

## 14. Questions and Appeals

### 14.1 Attendance Records
- Employees may request to review their attendance record
- Request to HR: Within 5 business days
- Records provided within 10 business days
- Employee may provide written explanation for absences

### 14.2 Disciplinary Appeals
- Employees may appeal disciplinary action
- Appeal to HR manager or next level management
- Appeal within 10 days of discipline
- Meeting held within 10 days
- Decision within 10 business days
