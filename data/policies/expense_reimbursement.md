# Expense Reimbursement Policy

**Document ID:** POL-004  
**Last Updated:** January 2024  
**Version:** 1.8

## 1. Purpose and Overview

This Expense Reimbursement Policy provides guidelines for employees to request reimbursement for reasonable business expenses incurred while performing their job duties. The policy ensures that employees are reimbursed fairly while maintaining fiscal responsibility.

## 2. General Principles

### 2.1 Reimbursable Expenses
The company will reimburse employees for reasonable and necessary business expenses, including:
- Travel expenses (flights, hotels, car rentals, mileage)
- Meals during business travel
- Conference and training registration fees
- Client entertainment and meals
- Office supplies and materials
- Telecommunications and technology costs
- Professional development and certifications

### 2.2 Non-Reimbursable Expenses
The company will not reimburse:
- Personal commuting or regular transportation
- Childcare or dependent care
- Personal grooming or clothing
- Traffic violations or parking tickets
- Alcoholic beverages (except when entertaining clients as approved)
- Personal entertainment (movies, music, books)
- Home office equipment (unless pre-approved)
- Losses due to negligence or unauthorized activities

### 2.3 Approval Requirements
All business expenses should be:
- Pre-approved by the employee's manager when possible
- Necessary for conducting company business
- Reasonable in amount and nature
- Properly documented with receipts
- Submitted within 30 days of incurring the expense

## 3. Travel Expenses

### 3.1 Travel Authorization
Employees must receive approval from their manager before traveling on company business. Authorization includes:
- Purpose of travel
- Destination and dates
- Estimated expenses
- Expected outcomes

### 3.2 Airfare and Transportation
- Employees should book flights in advance to obtain the best rates
- Economy or coach class is the standard for flights under 6 hours
- Business or premium economy may be approved for flights over 6 hours or for executives
- Non-refundable fares are preferred to reduce costs
- Employees should use company-preferred airlines and travel partners when available

### 3.3 Rental Cars
- Mid-size or standard rental cars are the standard
- Luxury or premium vehicles are not reimbursable
- Employees are responsible for any traffic violations or accidents
- Basic insurance coverage is provided; additional coverage should not be purchased
- Gas should be filled before returning the vehicle

### 3.4 Personal Vehicle Mileage
- Employees using personal vehicles for business travel will be reimbursed at the current IRS mileage rate
- The mileage rate is updated annually (currently $0.67 per mile)
- Mileage should be documented with a trip log including date, destination, and business purpose
- Parking and toll fees are reimbursable separately

### 3.5 Lodging
- Standard hotel rooms are reimbursable at prevailing rates for the destination
- Luxury or resort hotels are generally not reimbursable
- Incidentals (gratuities, internet, phone calls) are reimbursable
- Mini-bar, movie, and entertainment charges are not reimbursable

### 3.6 Meals During Travel
- Reasonable meal expenses are reimbursable during business travel
- Alcohol is reimbursable only when with clients or colleagues (not exceeding $30 per person per meal)
- Business meals should have a documented business purpose
- Meal receipts are required for expenses over $25
- Employees should not claim meal per diem if meals are provided

### 3.7 Ground Transportation
- Taxis, rideshare services, and public transportation are reimbursable
- Valet parking is reimbursable only for business purposes at conferences or events
- Airport parking is reimbursable

## 4. Conference and Training Expenses

### 4.1 Conference Registration
Conference registration fees are reimbursable for job-related professional development. Employees should:
- Obtain manager approval before registering
- Register in advance to obtain early-bird rates when available
- Provide conference materials and takeaways to share learning with the team

### 4.2 Travel to Conferences
Travel expenses to conferences, including flights, hotels, and meals, are reimbursable following the travel expense guidelines outlined above.

### 4.3 Training and Education
Costs for job-related training, certifications, and professional development are reimbursable with manager approval. This includes:
- Online courses
- Seminars and workshops
- Professional certification exams
- Textbooks and materials

### 4.4 Professional Memberships
Annual professional memberships are reimbursable for memberships relevant to the employee's role, subject to manager approval.

## 5. Client Entertainment and Meals

### 5.1 General Guidelines
Reasonable client entertainment and meal expenses are reimbursable when the purpose is to:
- Build business relationships
- Conduct business discussions
- Close or pursue business opportunities

### 5.2 Meal Entertainment
- Meals with clients should be at appropriate venues for business purposes
- Alcohol is permitted (not exceeding $30 per person)
- Gratuities should be 15-20% of the bill
- The business purpose should be documented (who attended, what was discussed)

### 5.3 Entertainment Events
- Tickets to sporting events, theater, or concerts may be reimbursed when there is a clear business purpose
- Cost should be reasonable for the market and event type
- Alcohol and food at events are reimbursable as part of the cost

### 5.4 Documentation
All client entertainment expenses require documentation including:
- Date and location of the meal or event
- Attendees and their companies
- Business purpose of the meal/event
- Itemized receipt

## 6. Office Supplies and Equipment

### 6.1 Office Supplies
Reasonable office supplies necessary for work are reimbursable, including:
- Pens, paper, notebooks
- Printing and copying
- Postage and shipping
- Office furniture (subject to pre-approval)

### 6.2 Technology Equipment
Technology equipment purchases require pre-approval and may include:
- Laptops or computers (company provides)
- Monitors, keyboards, mice (may be reimbursed up to $300)
- Software licenses and subscriptions
- Internet service for remote workers (up to $50/month)

### 6.3 Depreciation
For equipment with a useful life over one year, depreciation or capitalization guidelines will apply. Contact Finance for guidance.

## 7. Professional Development

### 7.1 Books and Materials
Books, journals, and professional materials relevant to the employee's role are reimbursable.

### 7.2 Certifications and Licenses
Costs for obtaining or renewing professional certifications and licenses required for the job are reimbursable.

### 7.3 Continuing Education
Tuition and fees for job-related continuing education are reimbursable with manager and HR approval. The company may require:
- A minimum grade or pass rate
- A commitment to remain employed for a specified period after completion
- Sharing of knowledge or certifications with the team

## 8. Submission and Approval Process

### 8.1 Submitting Expenses
Employees should submit expense reports:
- Within 30 days of incurring expenses
- Using the company's expense management system or form
- With original itemized receipts for all expenses
- With clear descriptions of business purpose

### 8.2 Receipt Requirements
- Receipts must show vendor name, date, and itemized description of charges
- Credit card statements alone are not sufficient documentation
- For expenses under $25, receipts are recommended but not required
- For expenses over $75, original itemized receipts are required

### 8.3 Reimbursement Process
1. Employee submits expense report with receipts
2. Manager reviews and approves within 5 business days
3. Finance department processes for accuracy and policy compliance
4. Reimbursement is made via paycheck or direct deposit within 2 pay periods

### 8.4 Denied Expenses
If an expense is denied:
- Finance will notify the employee in writing with an explanation
- The employee may appeal the decision to the Finance Manager
- The company will work with the employee to resolve disputes

## 9. Personal vs. Business Expenses

### 9.1 Mixed Expenses
When an expense includes both personal and business components:
- Only the business portion will be reimbursed
- The employee should separate charges when possible
- Clear documentation of the business use is required

### 9.2 Personal Travel
Personal travel expenses are never reimbursable, even if business is conducted during the trip.

## 10. International Travel

### 10.1 Currency and Exchange
- Expenses should be reported in USD using the exchange rate at the time of purchase
- Business travelers should use the company corporate credit card when possible to manage exchange rates

### 10.2 Visa and Passport
- Business visa fees are reimbursable
- Passport fees are generally not reimbursable (considered personal)
- Passport renewal required for business travel may be partially reimbursed with manager approval

### 10.3 Travel Insurance
- Travel insurance is reimbursable for international business travel
- Insurance should be arranged through company-approved providers

## 11. Corporate Credit Card

### 11.1 Credit Card Use
Employees may be issued a company corporate credit card for business expenses. Users must:
- Only use the card for business purposes
- Reconcile charges monthly with expense reports
- Report lost or stolen cards immediately
- Not share the card with others

### 11.2 Personal Charges
Personal charges on the corporate card will result in:
- Immediate payment required from the employee
- Disciplinary action
- Potential card suspension or revocation

## 11.3 Reconciliation
All corporate card charges must be reconciled with supporting expense reports within 30 days.

## 12. Special Circumstances

### 12.1 Per Diem
For extended travel assignments (typically over 30 days), the company may provide per diem instead of reimbursing individual meal expenses. Per diem rates are based on GSA guidelines for the destination.

### 12.2 Relocation Assistance
Employees relocating for a job may be eligible for relocation assistance. Contact HR for details.

### 12.3 Emergency Expenses
Employees incurring unexpected business expenses should:
- Document the expense thoroughly
- Notify their manager immediately
- Submit the expense report as soon as possible
- Emergency expenses are evaluated case-by-case

## 13. Policy Violations and Consequences

### 13.1 False Reporting
Submitting false or fraudulent expense reports is:
- A violation of company policy
- Grounds for immediate disciplinary action
- Potentially illegal
- Subject to criminal prosecution

### 13.2 Excessive Expenses
Patterns of excessive or inappropriate expenses may result in:
- Denial of future reimbursements
- Requirement for pre-approval of all expenses
- Formal disciplinary action
- Termination of employment

### 13.3 Appeals
Employees who believe an expense was improperly denied may appeal to the Finance Manager within 10 days of denial.

## 14. Responsibilities

### 14.1 Employee Responsibilities
- Incur only necessary business expenses
- Obtain proper approvals
- Maintain detailed receipts and documentation
- Submit reports promptly and accurately
- Follow company policy

### 14.2 Manager Responsibilities
- Pre-approve travel and significant expenses
- Review and approve submitted reports promptly
- Ensure policy compliance
- Coach employees on appropriate expenses

### 14.3 Finance Responsibilities
- Process reports accurately and promptly
- Verify policy compliance
- Maintain expense records
- Respond to questions and appeals

## 15. Questions and Support

For questions about the Expense Reimbursement Policy, please contact:
- Your manager for approval questions
- The Finance department at finance@company.com
- HR department at hr@company.com for policy questions
