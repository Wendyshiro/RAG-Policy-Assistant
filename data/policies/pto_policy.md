# Paid Time Off (PTO) Policy

**Document ID:** POL-001  
**Last Updated:** January 2024  
**Version:** 2.0

## 1. Overview

Our company is committed to providing employees with adequate time to rest, recharge, and attend to personal matters. This Paid Time Off (PTO) policy outlines the guidelines for requesting and using PTO, including vacation days, sick leave, and personal days.

## 2. PTO Eligibility

All full-time employees are eligible for PTO after their first 90 days of employment. Part-time employees (working 20+ hours per week) receive prorated PTO. Temporary and contract employees are not eligible for PTO unless otherwise specified in their employment agreement.

## 3. Annual PTO Accrual

### Full-Time Employees
- **Years 0-2:** 15 days per year (120 hours)
- **Years 3-5:** 20 days per year (160 hours)
- **Years 6+:** 25 days per year (200 hours)

### Part-Time Employees
PTO is calculated on a prorated basis based on hours worked. For example, an employee working 30 hours per week receives 75% of the full-time PTO allocation.

## 4. Types of PTO

### 4.1 Vacation Days
Vacation days are used for leisure, travel, and personal relaxation. Employees should plan vacation requests at least two weeks in advance when possible.

### 4.2 Sick Leave
Sick leave is provided for employees who are ill, need medical appointments, or need to care for sick family members. Employees should notify their manager as soon as possible when taking sick leave, ideally before the start of the workday.

### 4.3 Personal Days
Personal days can be used for any reason at the employee's discretion. These include attending to personal matters, family obligations, or other needs. Personal days should be requested with at least one week's notice when feasible.

## 5. PTO Accrual and Carryover

- PTO accrues on a monthly basis starting from the employee's eligibility date.
- Employees may carry over up to 5 days of unused PTO to the following calendar year.
- Any unused PTO beyond the carryover limit will be forfeited at the end of each calendar year.
- Upon termination of employment, employees will be paid out for all accrued and unused PTO at their current rate of pay.

## 6. Requesting PTO

### 6.1 Request Procedure
1. Submit PTO requests through the company's HR management system or to your direct manager
2. Provide at least two weeks' notice for vacation requests when possible
3. Provide as much notice as possible for unplanned absences (sick leave)
4. Ensure coverage for your responsibilities before taking extended time off

### 6.2 Approval Process
- Requests will be reviewed and approved by your direct manager
- Approval is at the discretion of management but will not be unreasonably withheld
- In cases of conflict, seniority and business needs will be considered
- Employees will be notified of approval status within 3 business days

### 6.3 PTO During Peak Periods
During company-identified peak business periods (typically Q4), PTO requests may be restricted. Employees will be notified of these periods in advance, typically 60 days before the period begins.

## 7. Sick Leave Guidelines

- **Notification:** Employees should notify their manager before 9:00 AM on the day they are taking sick leave
- **Medical Documentation:** For absences exceeding 3 consecutive days, a doctor's note may be required
- **Remote Work:** If an employee is capable of working remotely while ill, they may do so with manager approval
- **Abuse Prevention:** Excessive use of sick leave may trigger a performance discussion with HR

## 8. Holidays

In addition to PTO, all full-time employees receive the following paid holidays:
- New Year's Day
- Martin Luther King Jr. Day
- Presidents' Day
- Memorial Day
- Independence Day
- Labor Day
- Thanksgiving (2 days)
- Christmas Day

Holiday pay will be calculated at the employee's regular rate of pay.

## 9. Bereavement Leave

Employees are entitled to up to 5 days of paid bereavement leave in the event of the death of an immediate family member (spouse, child, parent, sibling). Additional unpaid leave may be granted at management's discretion.

## 10. Jury Duty and Court Appearances

Employees who are required to serve on jury duty or appear in court are entitled to paid time off for the duration of their service. Employees should notify HR as soon as they receive a summons.

## 11. Military Leave

Employees called to active military duty are entitled to unpaid leave in accordance with the Uniformed Services Employment and Reemployment Rights Act (USERRA). Employees should notify HR immediately upon receiving notification of deployment.

## 12. Unpaid Leave of Absence

Employees may request unpaid leave of absence for medical reasons, family obligations, or other personal matters. Requests must be submitted to HR and may be approved at the company's discretion. Employees on approved unpaid leave may maintain health insurance benefits by paying their portion of premiums.

## 13. Return from PTO

Employees are expected to return to work ready and able to perform their job duties. Any extensions to approved PTO must be requested immediately and approved before the original end date.

## 14. PTO and Termination

- Upon voluntary resignation, employees will receive payment for all accrued and unused PTO
- Upon termination for cause, employees will receive payment for accrued PTO as required by state law
- Final PTO payment will be included in the employee's final paycheck

## 15. Changes to PTO Policy

The company reserves the right to modify this PTO policy at any time with 30 days' notice to employees. Changes will be communicated via email and posted on the company intranet.
