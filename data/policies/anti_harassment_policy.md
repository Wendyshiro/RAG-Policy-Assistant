# Anti-Harassment and Discrimination Policy

**Document ID:** POL-007  
**Last Updated:** January 2024  
**Version:** 2.0

## 1. Purpose and Scope

This Anti-Harassment and Discrimination Policy reaffirms the company's commitment to maintaining a workplace free from harassment and discrimination. This policy applies to all employees, contractors, vendors, clients, and visitors to company facilities.

## 2. Non-Discrimination Policy

### 2.1 Statement
The company is an Equal Employment Opportunity (EEO) employer. We do not discriminate against employees or applicants based on:
- Race or color
- Religion or religious belief
- Sex (including pregnancy, gender identity, and sexual orientation)
- National origin
- Age (40 or older)
- Disability
- Genetic information
- Veteran status
- Political affiliation
- Or any other legally protected characteristic

### 2.2 Scope of Protection
This policy covers:
- Hiring and recruitment
- Compensation and benefits
- Job assignments and advancement
- Training and development
- Performance evaluation and discipline
- Termination of employment
- All other terms and conditions of employment

## 3. Harassment Policy

### 3.1 Definition
Harassment is unwelcome conduct, comment, gesture, or physical contact based on a protected characteristic that:
- Is severe, persistent, or pervasive
- Creates an intimidating, hostile, or offensive work environment
- Interferes with job performance or employment
- Is made an explicit or implicit term of employment

### 3.2 Examples of Harassment
Prohibited harassment includes, but is not limited to:
- Offensive jokes, slurs, or demeaning comments about protected characteristics
- Display of offensive materials, images, or symbols
- Unwelcome physical contact or gestures
- Intimidation, threats, or coercion
- Exclusion from work-related activities or social events
- Circulation of derogatory emails or messages
- Retaliation for reporting harassment
- Mocking someone's accent, religion, or cultural practices

### 3.3 Sexual Harassment
Sexual harassment is unwelcome conduct of a sexual nature including:
- Unwelcome sexual advances
- Requests for sexual favors
- Verbal harassment (wolf whistles, sexual comments)
- Physical harassment (unwanted touching, blocking pathways)
- Display of sexually explicit material
- Sexual jokes or innuendo
- Quid pro quo harassment (advancement in exchange for sexual favors)
- Hostile work environment due to sexual conduct

Even a single instance of severe sexual harassment may constitute a violation.

## 4. Retaliation Policy

### 4.1 Prohibition on Retaliation
The company strictly prohibits retaliation against any employee for:
- Filing a complaint about harassment or discrimination
- Participating in an investigation
- Refusing to participate in conduct that violates this policy
- Providing information to government agencies
- Objecting to or opposing discriminatory conduct

### 4.2 Protected Activities
Employees are protected when they:
- Report suspected harassment or discrimination
- Cooperate with investigations
- Testify in investigations or legal proceedings
- Refuse to engage in discriminatory conduct

### 4.3 Consequences of Retaliation
Any retaliation against employees who engage in protected activities will result in disciplinary action up to and including termination, regardless of the outcome of the underlying complaint.

## 5. Complaint and Reporting Procedures

### 5.1 When to Report
Employees who experience or witness harassment or discrimination should report it as soon as possible, ideally within 5 business days of the incident.

### 5.2 How to Report
Employees may report harassment or discrimination to:
- Their direct manager
- Their manager's manager
- HR department
- Compliance hotline
- Anonymous reporting portal

**Contact Information:**

- Compliance Hotline: 1-800-COMPLIANCE (24/7)
- Online Portal: [Compliance Portal URL]
- In-person: HR Office, [Location]

### 5.3 What to Include in a Report
When reporting, provide:
- Description of the incident(s) in detail
- Date, time, and location
- Names of individuals involved
- Names and contact information of witnesses
- Any documentation (emails, messages, photos)
- Previous incidents (if applicable)
- Impact on work and well-being

### 5.4 Confidentiality
Reports will be kept confidential to the extent possible. However, the company may be required to disclose information:
- To investigate the complaint
- To prevent imminent harm
- As required by law
- In response to legal proceedings

## 6. Investigation Process

### 6.1 Investigation Timeline
1. **Report received**: HR acknowledges within 24 hours
2. **Initial assessment**: Within 3 business days
3. **Investigation start**: Within 5 business days
4. **Investigation completion**: Within 30 days (or notify of delays)
5. **Findings communication**: Within 5 business days of completion

### 6.2 Investigation Procedures
The HR department will:
- Document all complaints in writing
- Interview the complainant
- Interview the respondent (alleged harasser)
- Interview witnesses with relevant information
- Review documentation and evidence
- Remain impartial and objective
- Maintain confidentiality throughout

### 6.3 Interim Measures
During investigation, the company may:
- Separate the parties involved
- Modify work schedules or assignments
- Place accused on administrative leave
- Restrict access to certain areas
- Provide support services to complainant

### 6.4 Remedial Actions
If harassment or discrimination is substantiated, the company will:
- Take appropriate corrective action
- Ensure the harassment stops
- Prevent retaliation
- Restore any lost benefits or compensation
- Document the investigation and action taken

## 7. Disciplinary Action

### 7.1 Progressive Discipline
For substantiated violations, the company typically imposes:
- First offense: Verbal warning and mandatory training
- Second offense: Written warning
- Third offense: Suspension
- Serious violations: Immediate termination

### 7.2 Serious Violations
The following violations may result in immediate termination:
- Sexual harassment or assault
- Racial or ethnic slurs
- Threats or intimidation
- Physical violence
- Repeated harassment after warning
- Retaliation against complainant

### 7.3 Non-Punishment
Disciplinary action for harassment is separate from the complaint process. Findings in a harassment investigation do not preclude civil or criminal action.

## 8. Employee Responsibilities

### 8.1 Expected Conduct
All employees are expected to:
- Treat all individuals with respect and dignity
- Not engage in or tolerate harassment or discrimination
- Refrain from offensive comments or conduct
- Report violations when witnessed
- Cooperate with investigations
- Not retaliate against others for reporting

### 8.2 Training Requirements
- All employees: Annual anti-harassment training (required)
- Managers: Annual anti-harassment and leadership training
- HR staff: Complaint handling and investigation training
- New hires: Anti-harassment training within 30 days

## 9. Managerial Responsibilities

### 9.1 Manager Obligations
Managers are responsible for:
- Creating a respectful work environment
- Ensuring this policy is communicated to their team
- Addressing inappropriate behavior immediately
- Reporting all complaints to HR
- Not investigating complaints themselves
- Supporting affected employees
- Maintaining confidentiality

### 9.2 Failure to Report
Managers who fail to report known harassment or discrimination may face disciplinary action, including termination.

Employees engaging in discriminatory or harassing conduct on social media may be subject to discipline if:

- The conduct damages company reputation

- May result in transfer or disciplinary action
- Must be disclosed to HR immediately
- May violate harassment policy if not consensual

## 15. Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What if I'm unsure whether something is harassment?**  
A: When in doubt, report it. HR will assess whether it violates policy. Good-faith reports made without intent to harm are protected.

**Q: What if the harasser is my manager?**  
A: Report to HR, the manager's manager, or the compliance hotline. You have multiple reporting channels.

**Q: Can I be fired for filing a complaint?**  
A: No. Retaliation is prohibited and may result in the harasser being fired instead.

**Q: How long is the investigation process?**  
A: Typically 30 days. Complex cases may take longer. You'll be updated on progress.

**Q: What if I'm afraid the harassment will continue during the investigation?**  
A: Tell HR. We can implement interim measures to separate you from the alleged harasser.
