# Drug and Alcohol Testing Policy

**Document ID:** POL-013  
**Last Updated:** January 2024  
**Version:** 1.4

## 1. Purpose and Scope

This Drug and Alcohol Testing Policy outlines the company's approach to substance abuse prevention and testing. The policy applies to all employees, applicants, and contractors.

## 2. Substance Abuse Policy

### 2.1 Prohibition
The company prohibits:
- Being under the influence of drugs or alcohol at work
- Using, possessing, or distributing illegal drugs at work or on company property
- Misuse of prescription medications at work
- Reporting to work impaired or unable to perform
- Manufacturing, growing, or cultivating illegal drugs
- Selling or distributing drugs or alcohol

### 2.2 Definition of Impairment
Being impaired includes:
- Inability to perform job duties safely
- Loss of coordination or balance
- Slurred speech or inability to concentrate
- Behavioral changes (aggression, paranoia, etc.)
- Odor of alcohol or marijuana
- Admission of substance use

## 3. Testing Policy

### 3.1 Types of Testing

**Pre-Employment Testing:**
- Conducted as condition of employment
- Before first day of work
- For all applicants (including contractors)
- Results reviewed before hiring

**Post-Offer Testing:**
- After job offer extended (conditional)
- Before employment begins
- Applies to all positions
- Does not delay start date

**Reasonable Suspicion Testing:**
- When manager suspects impairment
- Requested when specific behaviors observed
- Conducted at company expense
- Documentation of suspected behavior required

**Post-Incident Testing:**
- After workplace accident or injury
- Within a specified timeframe (typically 8-24 hours)
- Determines if substance use contributed
- Required for certain safety-sensitive roles

**Random Testing:**
- Unannounced and unscheduled
- Applied fairly to random selection
- Safety-sensitive positions more frequently
- All employees subject to potential testing

**Return-to-Duty Testing:**
- Required after substance abuse rehabilitation
- Before return from suspension
- Tests negative result must be obtained
- Successful completion of treatment required

**Follow-Up Testing:**
- Continuing surveillance after rehabilitation
- Unannounced, frequent testing
- May continue for up to 5 years
- Demonstrates commitment to sobriety

### 3.2 Prohibited Substances
Testing screens for:
- Alcohol (for safety-sensitive roles)
- Marijuana/Cannabis (including legal states)
- Cocaine
- Methamphetamine
- Opioids
- Amphetamines
- Phencyclidine (PCP)
- Barbiturates
- Benzodiazepines (if prescribed, disclose)

### 3.3 Medical Review Officer (MRO)
- Certified physician reviews all positive tests
- Contacts employee for explanation
- Considers legitimate prescribed medications
- Verifies accuracy of test result
- Determines if positive result valid
- Provides report to company

## 4. Testing Procedures

### 4.1 Testing Facility
- Testing conducted by certified laboratory
- DOT-certified laboratory required (if applicable)
- Testing facility meets federal standards
- Chain of custody procedures followed
- Results reported confidentially

### 4.2 Test Methods
- **Urine Testing**: Most common, screens for drugs
- **Breath Testing**: For alcohol (breathalyzer)
- **Saliva Testing**: Quicker results, screens for drugs
- **Hair Testing**: Longer detection window (up to 90 days)
- **Blood Testing**: Most accurate, invasive

### 4.3 Specimen Collection
- Witnessed collection for urine samples
- Witnessed collection for saliva samples
- Privacy respected during collection
- Collector trained in proper procedures
- Chain of custody maintained
- Employee may bring witness (own expense)

### 4.4 Split Specimen Option
- Employee may request split specimen
- Original specimen tested
- If positive, split specimen retained
- Employee may request independent testing of split
- Employee pays for independent test
- Results received within set timeframe

## 5. Positive Test Results

### 5.1 Notification
- Employee notified by Human Resources
- Results kept confidential
- Medical Review Officer speaks with employee
- Employee given opportunity to explain
- Consideration of prescription medications

### 5.2 Prescribed Medications
- Employee must disclose prescribed medications
- MRO considers legitimate prescriptions
- Medications taken as prescribed: Not considered positive
- Medications misused or abused: Considered positive
- Controlled medications: May require special handling

### 5.3 Positive Result Confirmation
- Positive test results confirmed by second test
- Gas chromatography/mass spectrometry (GC/MS) confirmation
- MRO reviews confirmatory results
- Employee notified of confirmed result

## 6. Consequences and Discipline

### 6.1 Pre-Employment Positive Result
- Job offer withdrawn
- Individual not hired
- Record of test kept confidential
- May reapply after specified period
- Substance abuse treatment may be required to reapply

### 6.2 Post-Employment Positive Result
- Immediate suspension of duties (if safety-sensitive)
- Meeting with HR and manager
- Options discussed:
  - Termination of employment
  - Enrollment in EAP/substance abuse treatment
  - Return-to-duty agreement
  - Suspension pending treatment

### 6.3 First Offense (Non-Safety-Sensitive Role)
- Written warning placed in file
- Requirement to enroll in EAP
- Drug testing participation required
- Successfully complete treatment program
- Follow-up testing for 12 months minimum
- Continued employment if conditions met

### 6.4 First Offense (Safety-Sensitive Role)
- Immediate suspension (with pay initially)
- Referral to substance abuse treatment program
- Medical evaluation required
- Enrollment in rehabilitation program
- Treatment completion required for reinstatement
- Return-to-duty testing and agreement
- Follow-up testing for 24 months

### 6.5 Positive Result on Follow-Up Test
- Violation of return-to-duty agreement
- Likely termination of employment
- Treatment contract may be breached
- Possible legal action

### 6.6 Refusal to Test
- Treated as positive result
- Disciplinary action taken
- Likely termination of employment
- Same consequences as positive test

## 7. Substance Abuse Treatment and Support

### 7.1 Employee Assistance Program
- Free, confidential counseling available
- Treatment referral service
- Treatment cost typically covered by insurance
- EAP coordinator: [Contact Information]
- Available 24/7

### 7.2 Treatment Options
- Inpatient/Residential Treatment
- Outpatient Treatment Programs
- Counseling and Therapy
- Peer Support Groups (AA, NA)
- Medication-Assisted Treatment
- Combination Approaches

### 7.3 Treatment Timeline
- Timely enrollment required (within 10 days)
- Program duration typically 30-90 days
- Follow-up care: 6-12 months recommended
- Return-to-duty only after completion
- Evidence of completion required

### 7.4 Voluntary Treatment
- Employees may seek treatment voluntarily
- Encouragement to use EAP
- Insurance coverage typically available
- No disciplinary action for voluntary treatment
- But substance-free workplace still required
- Return to work requires negative test

## 8. Privacy and Confidentiality

### 8.1 Confidentiality of Results
- Test results kept strictly confidential
- Only disclosed to authorized personnel
- Not discussed with coworkers
- Not posted or publicly shared
- Personnel file marked confidential
- Medical record created and segregated

### 8.2 Legal Requirements
- Testing complies with applicable laws
- Federal, state, and local regulations followed
- DOT regulations (if applicable)
- Medical privacy (HIPAA) respected
- Results protected from unauthorized access

### 8.3 Limited Access
- Results accessible to:
  - Employee tested
  - Medical Review Officer
  - HR manager
  - Legal counsel
  - Supervisor (if authorized)
  - Company medical staff
- Management does not see results
- Results not in personnel file

## 9. Misconduct and Discipline

### 9.1 Alcohol at Work
- Reporting to work with alcohol detected on breath: Suspension
- Possession of alcohol on company property: Violation
- Consumption of alcohol at work: Violation
- Being under the influence: Grounds for termination

### 9.2 Drug Use or Possession
- Possession of illegal drugs: Immediate termination
- Distribution of drugs: Immediate termination
- Manufacturing drugs: Immediate termination
- Being under influence: Suspension or termination
- Paraphernalia: Violation

## 10. Safety-Sensitive Positions

### 10.1 Definition
Safety-sensitive positions include:
- Operators of heavy machinery
- Commercial drivers
- Security personnel
- Healthcare workers
- Safety managers
- Supervisors in safety-critical areas
- Any role with accident potential

### 10.2 Enhanced Testing
Safety-sensitive positions subject to:
- Pre-employment testing (always required)
- Post-incident testing (always)
- Random testing (frequent, at least 50% annually)
- Return-to-duty testing (after any positive)
- Reasonable suspicion testing

### 10.3 Zero Tolerance
- Safety-sensitive positions: Zero tolerance for violations
- Positive test result: Termination typically
- Refusal to test: Termination
- Exception: Treatment program enrollment with special conditions
