# Performance Management and Evaluation Policy

**Document ID:** POL-011  
**Last Updated:** January 2024  
**Version:** 1.6

## 1. Overview and Purpose

This Performance Management and Evaluation Policy outlines the company's approach to evaluating employee performance, providing feedback, setting goals, and supporting employee development. The goal is to align employee performance with company objectives while identifying development opportunities.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:
- All employees (full-time and part-time)
- All departments and levels
- Does not apply to contractors or temporary workers

## 3. Performance Management Philosophy

The company believes in:
- Regular feedback and communication
- Goal-oriented performance management
- Employee development and growth
- Fair and objective evaluation
- Continuous improvement
- Recognition of achievement

## 4. Performance Review Cycle

### 4.1 Review Frequency
- **Annual Review**: Conducted once per year (typically December/January)
- **Mid-Year Check-in**: Informal discussion (June/July)
- **Ongoing Feedback**: Continuous throughout year
- **90-Day Review**: For new hires (end of probationary period)

### 4.2 Review Timeline
- **Annual Cycle**: January 1 - December 31
- **Review Period**: Typically December 15 - January 31
- **Notification**: Employees notified 30 days before review period
- **Completion Deadline**: January 31 of following year

### 4.3 Review Participants
- **Manager**: Primary evaluator
- **Employee**: Self-assessment
- **Peers**: Optional peer feedback (360 review)
- **Direct Reports**: If manager of others
- **HR**: Process oversight

## 5. Performance Evaluation Criteria

### 5.1 Competencies
All employees evaluated on core competencies:
- **Communication**: Clear, professional communication
- **Collaboration**: Works well with others
- **Problem-Solving**: Identifies and resolves issues
- **Initiative**: Takes action without prompting
- **Reliability**: Consistent, dependable, meets deadlines
- **Quality**: Produces high-quality work
- **Customer Service**: Supports customer/client needs
- **Continuous Learning**: Seeks growth and development

### 5.2 Job-Specific Competencies
Manager may evaluate additional competencies based on role:
- Technical skills and knowledge
- Leadership (if applicable)
- Sales or customer acquisition (if applicable)
- Project management (if applicable)
- Budget or resource management (if applicable)

### 5.3 Goals and Objectives
Employees typically have 3-5 goals:
- **Company Goals**: Alignment with company strategy
- **Department Goals**: Support for team objectives
- **Individual Goals**: Personal development and growth
- **Stretch Goals**: Growth-oriented goals

## 6. Goal-Setting Process (OKR or MBO)

### 6.1 Goal-Setting Timeline
- **Q4**: Goals discussed and drafted
- **January 1**: Goals finalized and documented
- **Quarterly**: Progress reviews (informal)
- **Mid-year**: Goals reviewed and adjusted as needed
- **December**: Final assessment against goals

### 6.2 SMART Goals
Goals should be:
- **Specific**: Clear, detailed description
- **Measurable**: Quantifiable or observable
- **Achievable**: Realistic and within capability
- **Relevant**: Aligned with job and company
- **Time-bound**: Completion date specified

### 6.3 Goal-Setting Meeting
Manager and employee meet to:
- Review company and department goals
- Discuss employee aspirations and interests
- Draft initial goals
- Ensure alignment and feasibility
- Document goals in writing
- Agree on success metrics

## 7. Performance Evaluation Process

### 7.1 Manager Preparation
Before the review meeting, manager:
- Reviews employee's entire year performance
- Gathers feedback from colleagues
- Reviews goals and accomplishments
- Notes specific examples and behaviors
- Considers peer feedback
- Prepares written evaluation
- Considers rating and justification

### 7.2 Employee Self-Assessment
Employee completes self-assessment including:
- Key accomplishments and projects
- Goals achieved
- Areas of growth
- Development needs and interests
- Skills gained or improved
- Challenges encountered
- Feedback for manager and team

### 7.3 Performance Rating Scale
Employees rated on typical scale:
- **5 - Exceptional**: Significantly exceeds expectations
- **4 - Exceeds Expectations**: Exceeds expectations consistently
- **3 - Meets Expectations**: Meets all job requirements
- **2 - Below Expectations**: Does not fully meet requirements
- **1 - Unsatisfactory**: Significantly below expectations

### 7.4 Performance Review Meeting
Manager and employee meet to:
- Discuss overall performance
- Review accomplishments and goals
- Review competencies and ratings
- Provide constructive feedback
- Listen to employee feedback and concerns
- Discuss development areas and interests
- Discuss goals for next year
- Discuss compensation (if applicable)
- Schedule follow-up

## 8. Feedback and Development

### 8.1 Ongoing Feedback
- Managers provide regular feedback (monthly minimum)
- Feedback timely, specific, and constructive
- Recognize strengths and achievements
- Address issues promptly
- One-on-one meetings scheduled regularly
- Documentation of significant feedback

### 8.2 Constructive Feedback
When providing feedback about areas for improvement:
- Use "SBI" model: Situation, Behavior, Impact
- Example: "In the meeting (situation), when you interrupted (behavior), it prevented others from contributing (impact)"
- Focus on behavior, not person
- Suggest specific improvements
- Ask for employee input
- Follow up on progress

### 8.3 Employee Development Plans
For employees needing improvement or seeking growth:
- Development goals identified
- Specific actions and milestones
- Timeline for achievement
- Support and resources provided
- Regular check-ins and feedback
- Progress reviewed at next evaluation

### 8.4 Training and Development
Company supports development through:
- Job-related training and courses
- Mentoring and coaching
- Cross-functional projects and assignments
- Conference attendance
- Online learning resources
- Tuition reimbursement
- Leadership development programs

## 9. Performance Improvement Plans (PIP)

### 9.1 When a PIP is Used
Performance Improvement Plans used when:
- Performance significantly below expectations
- Previous feedback has not resulted in improvement
- Specific competencies need improvement
- Behavioral issues need to be addressed
- Employee still has potential to succeed

### 9.2 PIP Components
A typical PIP includes:
- Specific performance deficiencies (with examples)
- Clear expectations and standards
- Specific, measurable improvement goals
- Actions employee must take
- Actions company will provide (coaching, training, resources)
- Timeline for improvement (typically 30-90 days)
- Metrics for success
- Consequences of not meeting PIP goals

### 9.3 PIP Process
1. Manager meets with employee to discuss PIP
2. HR reviews PIP criteria and process
3. PIP documented in writing and signed
4. Regular check-ins (weekly or bi-weekly)
5. Employee provided feedback on progress
6. Adjustments made as needed
7. Final evaluation at end of PIP period
8. Success: Goals met, PIP concluded
9. Non-success: Possible termination

### 9.4 PIP Documentation
PIP carefully documented:
- Clear explanation of performance deficiencies
- Specific examples of gaps
- Expected improvements
- Support provided
- Timeline and milestones
- Signed by employee, manager, and HR

## 10. Ratings and Compensation

### 10.1 Rating Distribution
- Ratings inform compensation decisions
- Exceptional performers: Merit increases and bonuses
- Meets expectations: Standard merit increase
- Below expectations: Limited or no increase
- Unsatisfactory: No increase (unless improved)

### 10.2 Compensation Discussion
- Compensation reviewed during performance evaluation
- Merit increases based on:
  - Performance rating
  - Market data for role
  - Budget availability
  - Tenure and progression
  - Market conditions
- Bonus/incentive eligibility discussed
- Promotion opportunities discussed

### 10.3 Equity and Fairness
- Performance evaluation conducted objectively
- Same standards applied across department
- Ratings reviewed for bias and consistency
- Market data considered
- Disparate impact analysis conducted
- Appeals process available

## 11. High Performers and Succession Planning

### 11.1 High Performer Identification
Employees rated 4-5 identified as:
- High potential for advancement
- Potential future leaders
- Subject matter experts
- Key resource retention

### 11.2 Succession Planning
For high performers:
- Stretch assignments offered
- Leadership training provided
- Mentoring by senior leaders
- Exposure to other departments
- Career path discussions
- Retention incentives considered

## 12. Performance Issues and Discipline

### 12.1 Progressive Discipline
Performance issues typically result in:
1. Coaching and feedback
2. Written warning
3. Performance Improvement Plan
4. Suspension or demotion
5. Termination

### 12.2 Bypassing Progressive Discipline
Serious issues may result in immediate termination:
- Theft or dishonesty
- Violation of safety rules
- Violence or threats
- Gross insubordination
- Violation of confidentiality
- Attendance abuse (no-call no-show pattern)

## 13. Appeals and Disputes

### 13.1 Performance Review Appeal
Employees may appeal their performance rating within 10 days:
- Submit written appeal to HR
- Explain disagreement with rating
- Provide documentation or evidence
- HR reviews appeal and rating
- HR meets with manager and employee
- Final decision made within 20 days

### 13.2 Appeal Resolution
Possible outcomes:
- Rating upheld
- Rating adjusted
- Additional feedback provided
- Further development planned
- Coaching or support offered

## 14. Confidentiality and Privacy

### 14.1 Review Confidentiality
- Performance reviews are confidential
- Shared with: Employee, manager, HR, management team
- Not discussed with other employees
- Not posted or publicly shared
- Secured in personnel file

### 14.2 Data Protection
- Performance data protected as personal data
- Subject to data privacy policies
- Employee may request copy
- Employee may provide written response
- Retained per document retention policy

## 15. Remote and Hybrid Employee Evaluations

### 15.1 Evaluation Consistency
Remote employees evaluated using same:
- Criteria and standards
- Goals and objectives
- Competencies
- Rating scale
- No bias against remote work arrangement

### 15.2 Feedback for Remote Employees
- Regular check-ins scheduled
- Video calls used for feedback
- Documentation of feedback provided
- Visibility into work quality maintained
- Opportunities for growth offered
