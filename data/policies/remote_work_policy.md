# Remote Work Policy

**Document ID:** POL-002  
**Last Updated:** January 2024  
**Version:** 1.5

## 1. Purpose

This Remote Work Policy establishes guidelines for employees working remotely to ensure productivity, security, and alignment with company objectives while maintaining a healthy work-life balance.

## 2. Eligibility

### 2.1 Who Can Work Remotely
- Full-time employees in roles that do not require daily in-office presence
- Employees who have completed their 90-day probationary period
- Employees with a satisfactory performance record
- Employees approved by their direct manager and department head

### 2.2 Who Cannot Work Remotely
- Employees in client-facing roles requiring in-person interaction
- Employees in manufacturing, warehouse, or facility-dependent roles
- New employees during their first 90 days (unless otherwise approved)
- Employees on a performance improvement plan (PIP)
- Interns and temporary workers

## 3. Remote Work Arrangements

### 3.1 Full-Time Remote
Employees may work fully remote from their chosen location (within the United States) with appropriate manager and HR approval. Full-time remote employees must maintain regular communication and be available during core business hours (9:00 AM - 5:00 PM in their local time zone).

### 3.2 Hybrid Remote
Employees work remotely part-time and are required to be in the office on specified days. The minimum office presence is typically 2 days per week, though this may vary by department.

### 3.3 Flexible Remote
Employees may work remotely on an as-needed basis with manager approval. Flexible remote arrangements should be coordinated to ensure adequate office coverage and team collaboration.

## 4. Request and Approval Process

### 4.1 Submitting a Request
1. Submit a Remote Work Request Form to your direct manager
2. Specify the desired remote work arrangement (full-time, hybrid, or flexible)
3. Explain how remote work will benefit both you and the company
4. Provide information about your proposed work environment and internet connectivity

### 4.2 Manager Review
Managers will review requests within 5 business days and consider:
- Job responsibilities and whether they can be performed remotely
- Team dynamics and collaboration needs
- Client or project requirements
- Employee performance and reliability

### 4.3 HR Approval
Final approval rests with HR. Approved arrangements will be documented in writing and provided to the employee.

### 4.4 Trial Period
New remote work arrangements may be subject to a 60-day trial period, after which both the employee and manager will evaluate the arrangement's success.

## 5. Work Schedule and Availability

### 5.1 Core Hours
Employees are expected to be available and working during core business hours:
- Eastern Time Zone: 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM
- Central Time Zone: 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM CT
- Mountain Time Zone: 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM MT
- Pacific Time Zone: 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM PT

### 5.2 Communication Expectations
- Employees must respond to messages and emails within 2 hours during core hours
- Regular check-ins with managers should be scheduled
- Attendance at required meetings (virtual or in-person) is mandatory
- Status updates should be provided as requested by management

### 5.3 Flexible Hours
Employees may negotiate flexible start and end times with their manager, provided core responsibilities are met and team collaboration is not compromised.

## 6. Equipment and Technology

### 6.1 Company-Provided Equipment
The company will provide:
- Laptop and peripherals (monitor, keyboard, mouse)
- Company phone
- Internet stipend (up to $50/month for home internet)
- VPN access and security software
- Collaboration tools and licenses

### 6.2 Employee Responsibilities
- Employees are responsible for maintaining a reliable internet connection (minimum 25 Mbps)
- Employees must keep company equipment secure and protected
- Employees must comply with all IT security policies
- Employees must not use personal devices for work without approval
- Employees should have a dedicated, quiet workspace for work

### 6.3 Equipment Maintenance
- Company IT department will provide technical support
- Employees should report equipment issues to IT within 24 hours
- Equipment must be returned to the company upon termination

## 7. Work Environment and Safety

### 7.1 Workspace Requirements
- Employees must have a dedicated workspace, separate from personal living areas when possible
- The workspace should be quiet, free from distractions, and professional
- Employees should ensure adequate lighting and ergonomic furniture
- Video calls should have a professional, neutral background

### 7.2 Ergonomics and Health
- Employees are responsible for maintaining an ergonomically sound workspace
- The company recommends ergonomic assessments for full-time remote workers
- Employees should take regular breaks and maintain work-life balance
- Employees should report any work-related injuries immediately to HR

### 7.3 Safety and Liability
- The company is not responsible for accidents or injuries in the employee's home
- Employees should ensure their workspace is free from hazards
- Employees working with hazardous materials must do so in the office

## 8. Security and Confidentiality

### 8.1 Data Security
- All company data must be accessed through the VPN
- Employees must use password-protected computers with encryption
- Personal information and confidential data should never be shared on unsecured networks
- Employees must lock their computer when stepping away from their desk

### 8.2 Confidentiality
- Employees must be mindful of confidentiality when discussing work in shared spaces
- Headphones should be used for confidential calls
- No client or company information should be shared in public spaces (coffee shops, etc.)
- Family members and household members should not have access to company data

### 8.3 Virtual Meeting Etiquette
- Employees should use backgrounds or virtual backgrounds if needed to maintain privacy
- Employees should mute microphones when not speaking
- Employees should avoid multitasking during meetings
- Recording meetings requires explicit consent from all participants

## 9. Attendance and In-Office Requirements

### 9.1 Hybrid Employees
Hybrid employees are typically expected to be in the office on Tuesdays and Thursdays, though this may vary by department. Schedule changes should be coordinated with the manager.

### 9.2 Company Events
All employees, including those who work remotely, are expected to attend:
- Annual company retreat (typically 2-3 days)
- Quarterly all-hands meetings (may be virtual)
- Department meetings and team building events (as scheduled)
- Important training and onboarding sessions

### 9.3 Client Meetings
If a remote employee has a client meeting scheduled, they should plan to either:
- Attend via video conference, or
- Travel to the office or client location as appropriate

## 10. Performance Management

### 10.1 Expectations
Remote employees are held to the same performance standards as in-office employees. The company will assess:
- Quality of work and meeting deadlines
- Communication and responsiveness
- Collaboration and team contribution
- Reliability and consistency

### 10.2 Monitoring
- The company may monitor productivity through approved tools
- Managers may request status updates or check-ins
- Employees should be transparent about their work and availability
- Privacy expectations are balanced with company security and productivity needs

### 10.3 Performance Issues
If a remote employee's performance declines, the company may:
- Require a temporary return to office work
- Adjust the remote work arrangement
- Implement a performance improvement plan
- Terminate the remote work arrangement

## 11. Termination of Remote Work Arrangement

Remote work arrangements may be terminated by either the employee or the company with 2 weeks' notice. Reasons for termination may include:
- Business needs requiring in-office presence
- Performance issues
- Violation of company policies
- Changes in job responsibilities
- Employee request

## 12. Travel and Work Location Changes

### 12.1 Relocation
If an employee relocates, they must notify HR immediately. The company may adjust compensation, benefits, or work arrangements based on the new location and applicable tax laws.

### 12.2 Travel While Remote
Employees traveling on business may continue working remotely provided they:
- Maintain reliable internet connectivity
- Comply with all security protocols
- Are available during core business hours
- Keep their manager informed of their location

### 12.3 Personal Travel
Employees are not permitted to work remotely from outside the United States without specific approval from HR, due to tax, legal, and data privacy considerations.

## 13. Expenses and Reimbursement

### 13.1 Internet and Phone
The company provides a monthly internet stipend of up to $50. Employees should submit receipts for reimbursement.

### 13.2 Equipment and Furniture
The company will reimburse approved equipment purchases up to $500 for initial setup. Additional furniture may be provided at the company's discretion.

### 13.3 Travel Expenses
Employees traveling to the office or company events should follow the standard expense reimbursement policy.

## 14. Continuity and Compliance

All remote employees must comply with:
- Company Code of Conduct
- Data Protection and Confidentiality Policies
- IT Security Policies
- Anti-Discrimination and Harassment Policies
- Attendance and Punctuality Expectations

## 15. Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I work remotely from a different country?**  
A: Generally, no. Working outside the United States may create tax and legal complications. Contact HR for approval in exceptional circumstances.

**Q: What if my internet goes down?**  
A: Notify your manager immediately. If you cannot work remotely due to technical issues, you should come to the office if possible.

**Q: Can I work from a coffee shop?**  
A: Occasionally, yes, but only for non-confidential work. Avoid public WiFi for sensitive data.

**Q: Are remote employees expected to socialize with coworkers?**  
A: Yes, informal virtual coffee chats and team events are encouraged. Remote employees should actively participate in team building.
