# Social Media and Internet Use Policy

**Document ID:** POL-012  
**Last Updated:** January 2024  
**Version:** 1.3

## 1. Purpose

This Social Media and Internet Use Policy provides guidelines for employee use of social media and internet during work hours. The policy balances company interests in productivity and brand protection with employee rights to limited personal use.

## 2. General Internet Use

### 2.1 Permitted Uses
Company internet and systems may be used for:
- Business purposes (required)
- Limited personal use during breaks
- Professional development and learning
- Job-related research and communication

### 2.2 Prohibited Uses
The following uses are strictly prohibited:
- Illegal activities
- Accessing illegal content (drugs, weapons, stolen goods)
- Sexually explicit or obscene material
- Gambling
- Hacking or unauthorized access
- Downloading or distributing copyrighted material
- Harassment or bullying
- Hate speech or discriminatory content
- Malware or virus distribution
- Competitive intelligence or espionage
- Excessive personal use that reduces productivity

### 2.3 Limited Personal Use
- Brief personal internet use permitted during breaks
- Should not interfere with work
- Should not consume excessive bandwidth
- Should not violate other policies
- Examples: Checking email, news, weather, social media (brief)
- Not permitted: Streaming video, gaming, online shopping

## 3. Monitoring and Privacy

### 3.1 No Privacy Expectation
Employees should understand that:
- Company systems are company property
- No expectation of privacy when using company systems
- Internet and email activity may be monitored
- Monitoring may be automatic or manual
- Monitoring done for security and compliance

### 3.2 Monitoring Methods
Company may monitor:
- Websites visited and time spent
- Email content and attachments
- File transfers and downloads
- Application usage
- Bandwidth consumption
- System access logs
- Chat and instant messaging

### 3.3 Monitoring Notice
- Employees notified of monitoring at hire
- Monitoring policy provided in handbook
- Reminders sent periodically
- Assumption: Continued work constitutes consent

### 3.4 Limited Privacy Areas
- Personal phone calls (some privacy expected)
- Personal email (may have some privacy)
- Counseling or medical discussions (confidential)
- Union activity (protected)
- Legally protected complaints (protected)

## 4. Email Use and Etiquette

### 4.1 Professional Communication
All email should be:
- Professional in tone and language
- Clear and concise
- Timely in response (within 24 hours typical)
- Respectful of others
- Free from discrimination or harassment
- Accurate and verified before sending

### 4.2 Email Retention
- Company email is retained per retention policy
- Employees should not delete emails to avoid legal hold
- Personal emails should be minimized
- Company data should not be sent to personal email
- Archive and delete unnecessary emails regularly

### 4.3 Email Security
- Strong passwords required
- Don't share passwords
- Lock device when away
- Don't access email on unsecured networks
- Use VPN for remote access
- Be cautious with attachments and links
- Report suspicious emails to IT

### 4.4 Confidential Information
- Confidential data should not be emailed unless necessary
- Use secure methods for sensitive information
- Encryption required for PII or health data
- Do not "reply all" with sensitive information
- Be aware of external recipients

## 5. Social Media Use During Work

### 5.1 Personal Social Media Accounts
- Limited personal social media use permitted during breaks
- Use personal accounts only (not company accounts)
- Use personal devices when possible
- Should not interfere with work
- Keep browsing brief
- Not permitted: Excessive use, streaming video

### 5.2 Company Social Media Accounts
- Only authorized employees may access
- Use company account credentials
- Follow company guidelines and brand standards
- Respond professionally to comments and inquiries
- Do not disclose confidential information
- Monitor for inappropriate comments
- Report negative or hostile comments
- Change passwords regularly

### 5.3 Authorization for Social Media
- Employees authorized to manage social accounts designated
- Written authorization from management required
- Training on social media guidelines required
- Account access documented and logged
- Handoff procedures for departing employees
- Regular review of posts and comments

## 6. Social Media and Personal Conduct

### 6.1 Personal Social Media Conduct
Employees' personal social media use should:
- Not violate company policies
- Not disclose confidential information
- Not represent company unless authorized
- Not disparage company or employees
- Not include discriminatory or harassing content
- Not create conflicts of interest
- Remember: Online conduct can reflect on company

### 6.2 Identifying Company Affiliation
When discussing work-related topics on personal social media:
- Use clear disclaimers ("views are my own")
- Do not represent company positions
- Do not claim to speak for company
- Do not use company logo or branding without permission
- Do not include company confidential information

### 6.3 Prohibited Personal Social Media Activity
Employees must not:
- Post company confidential information or trade secrets
- Disparage company in public posts
- Harass or bully coworkers online
- Engage in discriminatory or hateful speech
- Create accounts impersonating company or employees
- Use company name/logo without permission
- Violate customer or vendor confidentiality

### 6.4 Off-Duty Conduct
While company cannot control off-duty personal conduct:
- Conduct that damages company reputation subject to discipline
- Employees recognized as company representative
- Serious violations may result in termination
- Criminal activity always subject to discipline
- Discrimination or hate speech serious violations

## 7. Blogging and Public Writing

### 7.1 Personal Blogs
- Employees may maintain personal blogs off company time
- Use clear disclaimers (views are personal, not company)
- Do not disclose confidential information
- Do not identify clients or customers
- Do not disparage company
- Follow non-compete and non-solicitation agreements
- Consider company reputation

### 7.2 Company Blog or Writing
- Only authorized employees may write for company
- All posts subject to review and approval
- Follow company guidelines and brand standards
- Verify all information before publishing
- Include proper attribution and sources
- Do not disclose confidential information
- Maintain professional tone

### 7.3 News Articles and Interviews
- Interview requests referred to communications department
- Only authorized spokespersons speak to media
- Media training required before interviews
- Employees should not identify self as company spokesperson
- Company review of comments before publication preferred
- Clear disclaimers if speaking personally

## 8. Streaming and Bandwidth

### 8.1 Streaming Restrictions
- Streaming video not permitted during work
- Streaming audio permitted in limited amounts
- Podcasts and music acceptable during work (if not distracting)
- Excessive streaming impacts network performance
- Company may throttle or block streaming services
- Video streaming only in break rooms

### 8.2 Bandwidth Management
- Company monitors and manages bandwidth usage
- High-usage individuals may be cautioned
- Patterns of abuse result in disciplinary action
- Bandwidth restrictions may be implemented
- VPN usage may be limited due to bandwidth
- Game updates and large downloads restrict to off-hours

## 9. Online Security and Safety

### 9.1 Phishing and Scams
- Be alert to phishing emails and malicious links
- Do not click links from unknown senders
- Verify sender identity before clicking
- Report suspicious emails to IT immediately
- Do not download attachments from unknown sources
- Be cautious with shortened URLs

### 9.2 Password and Credential Security
- Never share passwords
- Never write down passwords
- Use strong, unique passwords
- Change passwords every 90 days
- Report compromised passwords immediately
- Multi-factor authentication required for sensitive systems

### 9.3 Malware and Viruses
- Do not download from untrusted sources
- Antivirus software required and must be active
- Report suspicious activity to IT
- Do not disable security software
- Keep systems patched and updated
- Back up important files regularly

## 10. Remote Work Internet Use

### 10.1 Remote Work Requirements
- Secure internet connection required
- Avoid public WiFi for company work
- VPN required for company system access
- Password-protected WiFi strongly recommended
- Wired connection preferred if available
- No sharing of login credentials with household members

### 10.2 Household Network Security
- Remote employee responsible for network security
- Wi-Fi should be encrypted and password-protected
- Other household members should not access company data
- Guest network recommended for household WiFi
- Network should be secured with strong password

## 11. Company Representation Online

### 11.1 Social Media Profiles
- Avoid using company logo as personal profile image
- If company affiliation listed, use clear disclaimers
- Do not claim official status unless authorized
- Be aware others will see company connection
- Consider professional standards for profile information

### 11.2 Company Announcements
- Only communications department authorizes announcements
- Do not announce company news on personal social media
- Company has right to announce news first
- Do not release information before official announcement
- Premature announcements may violate confidentiality

## 12. Acceptable Use Violations and Discipline

### 12.1 Discipline for Violations
Violations may result in:
- Verbal warning
- Written warning
- Suspension without pay
- Restriction of internet access
- Termination of employment
- Civil or criminal prosecution (for serious violations)

### 12.2 Serious Violations
Violations resulting in immediate termination:
- Accessing illegal content
- Downloading illegal material
- Hacking or unauthorized access
- Harassment or bullying
- Hate speech or discrimination
- Theft or misappropriation

## 13. Questions and Reporting

For questions about social media and internet use:
- **Manager**: Clarification about acceptable use
- **IT Department**: Technical questions and security concerns
- **HR Department**: Policy questions and concerns
- **Anonymous Hotline**: Report violations: 1-800-COMPLIANCE
