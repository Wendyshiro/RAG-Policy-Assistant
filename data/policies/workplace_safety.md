# Workplace Safety and Injury Prevention Policy

**Document ID:** POL-008  
**Last Updated:** January 2024  
**Version:** 1.7

## 1. Purpose and Commitment

The company is committed to providing a safe and healthy workplace for all employees, contractors, and visitors. This Workplace Safety and Injury Prevention Policy outlines our commitment to preventing workplace injuries and illnesses and establishing procedures for safe operations.

## 2. Scope and Applicability

This policy applies to:
- All employees (full-time and part-time)
- Contractors and temporary workers
- Volunteers and visitors
- All company facilities and work sites
- Remote work environments

## 3. Safety Responsibilities

### 3.1 Company Responsibilities
The company commits to:
- Complying with all OSHA regulations and safety standards
- Maintaining safe working conditions
- Providing safety training and equipment
- Investigating and addressing safety concerns
- Implementing corrective actions
- Maintaining workers' compensation insurance
- Promoting a safety-conscious culture

### 3.2 Manager Responsibilities
Managers must:
- Enforce all safety policies and procedures
- Conduct safety briefings and training
- Ensure protective equipment is available
- Investigate incidents and near-misses
- Take corrective action for violations
- Model safe behavior
- Encourage employees to report hazards

### 3.3 Employee Responsibilities
All employees must:
- Follow all safety policies and procedures
- Use required protective equipment
- Report hazards and near-misses
- Participate in safety training
- Cooperate with investigations
- Maintain awareness of surroundings
- Never work under the influence of drugs or alcohol
- Report injuries promptly

## 4. Safety Rules and Procedures

### 4.1 General Safety Rules
- All employees must comply with safety instructions and signage
- No running, horseplay, or distracting conduct in work areas
- Keep work areas clean and organized
- Report trip hazards, spills, or obstacles immediately
- Use stairs and walkways properly
- Keep emergency exits clear at all times
- Do not block emergency equipment or exits

### 4.2 Use of Protective Equipment
- Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) must be worn when required
- PPE types may include: hard hats, safety glasses, gloves, hearing protection, respirators, steel-toed boots
- Employees must wear PPE correctly and maintain it properly
- Damaged or defective PPE must be replaced immediately
- Management will provide all necessary PPE at no cost to employees

### 4.3 Lifting and Ergonomics
- Proper lifting techniques must be used for all manual lifting
- Items over 50 pounds: Use mechanical lifts or ask for assistance
- Bend at knees, keep back straight, avoid twisting
- Repetitive tasks: Take regular breaks and vary movements
- Report pain or discomfort to manager or occupational health

### 4.4 Equipment Operation
- Only trained and authorized employees may operate equipment
- All equipment must be inspected before use
- Safety guards must be in place and not removed
- Equipment must be shut down and locked out before maintenance
- Never operate equipment while fatigued or impaired

## 5. Hazard Communication and Assessment

### 5.1 Hazard Identification
- All workplace hazards must be identified and documented
- Chemical hazards: Safety Data Sheets (SDS) maintained and accessible
- Biological hazards: Proper containment and handling procedures
- Physical hazards: Guards and protective measures in place
- Ergonomic hazards: Workstation assessments conducted

### 5.2 Safety Data Sheets (SDS)
- All chemical products must have current SDS available
- SDS must be in employee-accessible locations
- Employees working with chemicals must review SDS before use
- SDS must include hazard information and emergency procedures

### 5.3 Hazard Reporting
Employees should report hazards immediately to:
- Direct manager
- Safety officer (if designated)
- HR department
- Anonymous safety hotline: [Phone Number]

Reports should include:
- Description of hazard
- Location and affected areas
- Potential consequences
- Suggested remediation

### 5.4 Hazard Assessment and Corrective Action
- All reported hazards will be assessed within 24 hours
- Interim measures taken to prevent injury
- Corrective actions completed within agreed timeframe
- Follow-up to verify effectiveness
- Employees informed of actions taken

## 6. Incident Reporting and Investigation

### 6.1 Injury Reporting
All workplace injuries, regardless of severity, must be reported to:
- Manager immediately or within 24 hours
- HR department or occupational health
- Workers' compensation administrator (if medical care required)

**Do not delay reporting due to concern about blame or discipline.**

### 6.2 Incident Report Form
Employees must complete an incident report including:
- Date, time, and location of incident
- Description of what happened
- Injuries sustained
- Names of witnesses
- Equipment or materials involved
- Causative factors
- Any corrective actions needed

### 6.3 Investigation Process
All incidents will be investigated by:
- Management and/or safety officer
- Focus: Identify root causes, not assign blame
- Interview employee, witnesses, and manager
- Document findings and corrective actions
- Complete investigation within 10 business days
- Provide findings to affected parties

### 6.4 Near-Miss Reporting
Employees should report near-miss incidents (potential injuries that didn't occur) using same process. Near-miss reporting helps prevent future injuries.

## 7. Medical Treatment and Return to Work

### 7.1 First Aid and Medical Treatment
- First aid kits available in all work areas
- AED (automated external defibrillator) on-site
- Trained first aid responders available
- Serious injuries: Call 911 immediately
- Employees may go to preferred medical provider

### 7.2 Workers' Compensation
- All employees covered by workers' compensation insurance
- Coverage includes medical treatment and wage replacement
- Employee must file claim through HR department
- No out-of-pocket cost for work-related injuries
- State workers' compensation board has jurisdiction

### 7.3 Workplace Accommodation and Return to Work
- Light duty assignments available for injured employees
- Graduated return-to-work programs
- Job modifications and accommodations as appropriate
- Manager and occupational health coordinate return
- Medical certification required before full-duty return

### 7.4 Retaliation Prevention
Employees receiving workers' compensation or reporting injuries will not face:
- Discipline or termination
- Reduced hours or shift changes
- Isolation or exclusion
- Negative performance evaluations
- Any adverse action

Retaliation is prohibited by law and company policy.

## 8. Safety Training

### 8.1 New Employee Training
All new employees receive:
- General workplace safety orientation (within first week)
- Job-specific safety training (before starting work)
- Hazard communication overview
- Emergency procedures and exits
- Incident reporting procedures
- Documentation of training completion

### 8.2 Ongoing Safety Training
- Annual refresher training for all employees
- Equipment-specific training before operation
- Hazard-specific training as needed
- Retraining when procedures change
- New hazard training when introduced

### 8.3 Safety Meetings
- Monthly safety meetings (department or facility-wide)
- Agenda: Incident review, hazards, procedures, best practices
- Attendance expected and documented
- Minutes recorded and distributed
- Action items tracked and followed up

## 9. Drugs, Alcohol, and Impairment

### 9.1 Substance Abuse Policy
- Being under the influence of drugs or alcohol at work is prohibited
- Employees must be capable of performing job duties safely
- Impairment includes: Illegal drugs, prescription misuse, alcohol, marijuana, over-the-counter medications

### 9.2 Testing and Screening
- Drug and alcohol testing may occur:
  - As a condition of employment
  - Upon reasonable suspicion of impairment
  - After workplace incidents
  - As part of random testing program
- Employees must comply with testing requests

### 9.3 Substance Abuse Assistance
- Employees with substance abuse issues are encouraged to seek help
- EAP provides confidential counseling and referral services
- Seeking help voluntarily will not result in termination
- Medical leave may be available for treatment
- Return-to-work agreement may be required

## 10. Violence Prevention

### 10.1 Zero Tolerance for Violence
The company has zero tolerance for workplace violence, including:
- Threats of violence
- Physical violence or assault
- Intimidation or coercion
- Weapons in workplace
- Aggressive or threatening behavior

### 10.2 Threat Assessment
- All threats will be taken seriously and investigated
- Assessment considers: Specificity, severity, immediacy, past behavior
- Interim measures: Separation, suspension, removal from premises
- Law enforcement may be contacted

### 10.3 Violent Incident Response
If violence occurs:
1. Ensure immediate safety (evacuate if necessary)
2. Call 911 for emergency services
3. Notify manager and HR immediately
4. Document what occurred
5. Preserve evidence
6. Cooperate with investigation

### 10.4 Weapons Policy
- No weapons of any kind permitted on company premises
- Exception: Authorized security personnel only
- Violation: Immediate termination and potential criminal prosecution

## 11. Emergency Preparedness

### 11.1 Emergency Procedures
- Evacuation procedures posted and communicated
- Fire extinguishers: Location and use training provided
- Emergency contact procedures established
- Shelter-in-place procedures available
- Medical emergency procedures established

### 11.2 Emergency Drills
- Fire drills: Quarterly minimum
- Other emergency drills: As needed
- Attendance required
- Documentation of participation
- Lessons learned reviewed and addressed

### 11.3 Emergency Assembly Points
- Designated assembly areas away from building
- Accountability procedures
- First aid station and emergency equipment
- All employees must know location

## 12. Workplace Inspections

### 12.1 Safety Inspections
- Management conducts monthly safety inspections
- OSHA-type audit checklist used
- Documentation of findings
- Corrective action plans for deficiencies
- Follow-up to verify corrections

### 12.2 Ergonomic Assessments
- Workstation assessments available upon request
- Assessment includes: Desk, chair, monitor, keyboard, desk height
- Recommendations for improvements
- Equipment modifications or purchases as needed

## 13. Contractor and Vendor Safety

### 13.1 Contractor Management
- Contractors must comply with this safety policy
- Safety requirements included in contracts
- Proof of insurance required
- Safety briefing before starting work
- Monitored for ongoing compliance

### 13.2 Vendor Safety
- Vendors delivering materials must follow safety procedures
- Loading/unloading: Supervised and safe procedures
- Vehicle safety: Proper parking, hazard lights
- Equipment safety: Secured and inspected

## 14. Disability and Accommodation

### 14.1 Accessibility
- Workplace is accessible to employees with disabilities
- Ramps, elevators, accessible restrooms provided
- Parking accommodations available
- Assistive technology available

### 14.2 Reasonable Accommodations
- Employees may request workplace accommodations
- Request to HR: Written preferred
- Assessment of feasibility and burden
- Good-faith discussion to find solutions
- Documentation of accommodation decision

## 15. Safety Communication

### 15.1 Safety Postings
- Safety posters and signage displayed
- OSHA required postings available
- Emergency procedures visible
- Hazard labels on chemicals and equipment
- Updated regularly

### 15.2 Safety Bulletins
- Weekly safety tip emails
- Department safety newsletters
- Safety reminders at department meetings
- Lessons learned from incidents

## 16. Compliance and Violations

### 16.1 Safety Violations
Violations of this policy may result in:
- Coaching and training
- Written warning
- Suspension without pay
- Termination
- Criminal charges (for serious violations)

### 16.2 Serious Violations
Immediate termination may result from:
- Violence or threats
- Weapons in workplace
- Substance abuse at work
- Operating equipment while impaired
- Gross negligence causing injury
- Repeated violations after warnings
